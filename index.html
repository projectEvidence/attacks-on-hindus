1. Hindu Couple Brutally Attacked and Killed in Cumilla; Allegations of Communal Motive and Cover-Up
Date of Incident:
December 25, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Comilla
Upazilla: Debidwar
Details
In a horrific act of communal violence, an elderly Hindu couple—Nitai Bhowmik and his wife—were brutally attacked and killed in their home in Barkamta village, Debidwar Upazila, Cumilla District, on the night of December 26, 2024, at around 8:15 PM.

The attackers, reportedly led by Md. Titu Swarnakar, son of Md. Yusuf Swarnakar, were part of a local Muslim gang that allegedly stormed the couple’s residence with knives and sticks, aiming to kill and loot their property. Both victims died on the spot due to the severe nature of the assault.

Shockingly, both the victims and the perpetrators are from the same village, yet instead of legal action being pursued, local BNP leaders are allegedly attempting to suppress the case through informal arbitration (salish)—a move widely condemned by human rights observers as an attempt to deny justice and protect the attackers.

Community members fear the assault was motivated not just by greed, but by religious hatred, as Hindu families in rural Bangladesh increasingly face targeted violence and systemic marginalization. Calls for justice have intensified as the family and locals demand criminal prosecution and government intervention, warning against any political protection or backdoor settlements.

This attack adds to the growing list of violent crimes against minorities that are often buried under silence, social pressure, or political influence—leaving victims without justice and communities in fear.

December 20, 2025

Details

4. Ancestral land of Satyaranjan Patni was forcefully grabbed defying court order in Chailabunia Village, Galachipa
Date of Incident:
December 7, 2025
Division: Barishal
District: Patuakhali
Upazilla: Galachipa
Details
In a disturbing example of disregard for legal justice and minority rights, a Hindu family in Chhaila Buniya village, Galachipa Upazila of Patuakhali, has accused local musclemen of forcibly seizing their land in defiance of a court verdict. Victim Satyaranjan Patni raised the alarm during a press conference at the Patuakhali Press Club on Sunday, joined by his family and legal counsel.

According to Satyaranjan, a 2021 inheritance case over 4.84 acres of ancestral land was settled in their favor by a local court in February 2023. An appeal was filed by the opposing party but was rejected by the Senior District Judge’s Court. Following that, government officials officially demarcated and handed over the land to the rightful owners.

Yet, in blatant contempt of the law, Md. Abdur Rob Hawlader and Mofez Uddin Sikder have allegedly taken over the land by force. On Sunday, they even harvested paddy from the disputed field, ignoring both the court order and the police. Despite repeated complaints, local authorities and even the military camp reportedly failed to act.

The accused claim to have bought the land from women in Satyaranjan’s extended family, but under Hindu family law, such female inheritance claims are invalid in this context. Still, these false claims are being used to justify the ongoing occupation and intimidation.

Satyaranjan and his family now live in fear, constantly receiving threats. Their lawyer stated, "My clients legally marked the land with court-decreed boundaries over a month ago, yet the occupiers continue to dominate with brute power. The family is terrified and urgently seeks protection and justice."

This incident reflects a worrying trend of land grabbing targeting religious minorities, where even court decrees fail to protect victims in the face of political influence and law enforcement inaction.

5. Hindu Kali Temple Vandalized in Netrokona; Community in Shock
Date of Incident:
December 4, 2025
Division: Mymensingh
District: Netrokona
Upazilla: Netrakona-S Upazilla
Specific Location: Sarvajanin Kali Temple, Laxmiganj Union
Details
An idol was vandalized in the Sarvajanin Kali Temple in Nasibpur village of Laxmiganj union in Netrokona Sadar upazila. The incident took place on Thursday (December 5) night. After learning about the incident on Friday morning, the police detained one person for questioning.

It is learned that 8-10 Hindu families in Nasibpur village have established a public temple in a tin hut and have been performing annual Kali Puja and daily puja for a long time. At some point on Thursday night, miscreants entered the temple and partially broke the hands of the Kali idol and the heads of the Dakini and Yogini idols. They also tried to set the Kali idol on fire by placing the hair on the floor. Although some of the hair burned, the fire did not spread around.

The residents of the village came to know about the matter on Friday morning. On receiving the information, Additional Superintendent of Police Saheb Ali Pathan, Sadar Upazila Executive Officer (UNO) Asma Binte Rafique, District Puja Celebration Committee President Gyanesh Sarkar, police, law enforcement and administration officials along with the police station visited the spot.

Eyewitnesses said that there was no lock on the door of the temple. It was in a very unprotected state. No one could say anything about who did this. A person named Nitai Sutradhar filed a complaint with the police station on behalf of the villagers.

District Puja Celebration Committee President Gyanesh Sarkar said that the incident is highly condemnable. Someone may have done this with the intention of tarnishing our communal harmony and the image of the government. He demanded a fair investigation into the matter.

Additional Superintendent of Police Saheb Ali Pathan said that the incident is being investigated. If evidence of anyone's involvement is found in the investigation, legal action will be taken. However, one person has been initially detained for questioning.

UNO Asma Binte Rafiq said that unknown assailants have partially broken some idols in the temple. Law enforcement agencies are investigating the incident. We have assured cooperation to the victims.

Details

8. Hindu Farmland in Rajshahi’s Puthia Targeted by BNP Leader for Pond Digging; Clashes Leave Several Injured
Date of Incident:
May 29, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Rajshahi
Upazilla: Puthia
Specific Location: Goragachhi Hindu Para, Shilmaria Union
Details
A tense and violent confrontation unfolded in Goragachhi Hindu Para, Silmaria Union, Puthia Upazila, Rajshahi, as a group allegedly led by local BNP leader Abul Kalam attempted to forcibly excavate a pond on land owned by Hindu farmers.

The incident occurred late Friday night, when villagers spotted heavy machinery (an excavator) brought in under cover of darkness. Local Hindu residents, fearing a land seizure, protested the excavation, leading to clashes that left at least five people injured, including workers brought to dig the pond.

According to villagers, Kalam and associates had forcibly acquired land deeds from some Hindu families using threats, while others refused. These lands were allegedly fraudulently transferred in the name of a local ruling-party leader, Dr. Sanjeeb, then leased back to Kalam for 10 years. Claiming administrative permission, Kalam tried to start pond digging—without any legal approval, according to the Puthia Upazila Nirbahi Officer (UNO).

In response, locals united and drove out the excavation team, vandalizing the illegal machinery and seizing locally made weapons found at the site.

Police were deployed the following morning to prevent further escalation, and officials confirmed that no permission had been granted for such excavation. Despite Kalam’s claims of legality, the incident is being widely seen as a coordinated attempt to encroach upon minority-owned agricultural land under political cover.

This case reflects a growing pattern of intimidation and land seizures targeting Hindu communities in rural Bangladesh. Local residents are demanding legal action, land restoration, and protection from politically backed aggression.

9. Mob Attacks Hindu Family in Barishal’s Koloshkathi; Home Looted, Temple Desecrated, Women Assaulted
Date of Incident:
May 5, 2025
Division: Barishal
District: Barishal
Police Station: Bakerganj
Specific Location: House of Khokon Das
Details
In a violent land-grabbing attack on May 6, 2024, a Hindu family's home and private temple were attacked, looted, and vandalized in Ward No. 1 of Koloshkathi Union, Bakerganj Upazila, Barishal District. The home belonged to the family of Bidhan Kumar Dash, who had been peacefully residing there for over 60 years.
