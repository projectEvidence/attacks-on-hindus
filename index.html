1. Robbers stabbed Hindu Gold merchant Sanjit Mondal (38) to a grisly state, looted gold ornaments and money in Konabari, Gazipur
Date of Incident:
July 2, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Gazipur
Upazilla: Gazipur Sadar-Joydebpur
Specific Location: Konabari College Gate area
Details
Sanjit Mondak (38), a gold merchant was robbed of gold ornaments and cash by a group of miscreants after stabbing him with a sharp weapon in Konabari, Gazipur around 10:30 pm on Thursday (July 3). The miscreants created panic by exploding a cocktail and fled. The victim is a resident of Dewaliabari area of ​​the Sadar upazila. In addition to his jewelry business, he was an agent of mobile financial services company (MFS) bKash.

Robbers robbed 5 bhori gold ornaments and 2 lakh taka. He is currently undergoing treatment at Enam Medical College Hospital in Savar.

Relatives and eyewitnesses of the injured said that Sanjit Mondal has a shop called Sathi Jewelers in Konabari College Gate School Market. In addition to the jewelry business, he also used to do bKash business in that shop. He was returning home with gold ornaments and cash after closing the shop at around 10:30 pm on Thursday. As soon as he reached College Gate after closing the shop, the miscreants suddenly blocked his path and stabbed him, injuring him. At that time, the miscreants snatched 5 bhoris of gold and 2 lakh taka of cash for bKash transactions from the bag he was carrying. When the nearby businessmen came forward hearing Sanjit's screams, the miscreants caused panic by exploding a cocktail and fled with the gold ornaments and cash. Later, locals rescued Sanjit and took him to the Konabari clinic for first aid.

Salah Uddin, officer-in-charge (OC) of Konabari Police Station of GMP (Gazipur Metropolitan Police), said, "After receiving the information about the robbery, the police went to the spot but did not find anyone. The police are conducting a drive to identify the robbers and arrest them. Legal action is under process in this regard."

3. Hindu College Girl Abducted in Habiganj: Family Pleads for Justice Amid Police Inaction
Date of Incident:
May 30, 2025
Division: Sylhet
District: Habiganj
Upazilla: Madhabpur
Details
In a heart-wrenching incident of communal oppression and gender-based violence, a bright Hindu college student named Monpriya Sarkar, a student of Shahjalal Government College in Madhabpur, Habiganj, was forcibly abducted by a local youth named Humayun and his associates. The incident took place at around 11 PM on the night of ১২ মাঘ (12th Magh), 1431 Bengali calendar, which corresponds to January 26, 2025 in the Gregorian calendar. Monpriya had gone outside her home briefly to use the bathroom when she was violently seized and taken away in the darkness, leaving her family devastated and terrified.

According to Monpriya’s grieving mother, the family immediately began searching and raising alarms. However, instead of assistance, they faced intimidation. The accused’s father, Umayun Mia, visited the family and pressured them not to go to the police, falsely promising that Monpriya would be returned within three days. When that didn’t happen, the promises turned into stalling tactics. The girl was neither produced in court nor returned to her home. The victim’s family was manipulated into silence while valuable time passed.

Eventually, after their pleas went unanswered, Monpriya’s parents turned to the legal system and filed a case against Humayun and his father. Yet, even after a week, there has been no progress, no arrest, and no trace of their daughter. The victim’s family believes the lack of action stems from the fact that they are Hindu, and the perpetrators belong to the Muslim majority, highlighting the ongoing issue of communal bias in law enforcement.

The family continues to cry out for justice, demanding the immediate recovery of Monpriya, strict punishment for the perpetrators, and state protection for vulnerable Hindu families facing such communal threats.

4. Hindu Farmland in Rajshahi’s Puthia Targeted by BNP Leader for Pond Digging; Clashes Leave Several Injured
Date of Incident:
May 29, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Rajshahi
Upazilla: Puthia
Specific Location: Goragachhi Hindu Para, Shilmaria Union
Details
A tense and violent confrontation unfolded in Goragachhi Hindu Para, Silmaria Union, Puthia Upazila, Rajshahi, as a group allegedly led by local BNP leader Abul Kalam attempted to forcibly excavate a pond on land owned by Hindu farmers.

The incident occurred late Friday night, when villagers spotted heavy machinery (an excavator) brought in under cover of darkness. Local Hindu residents, fearing a land seizure, protested the excavation, leading to clashes that left at least five people injured, including workers brought to dig the pond.

According to villagers, Kalam and associates had forcibly acquired land deeds from some Hindu families using threats, while others refused. These lands were allegedly fraudulently transferred in the name of a local ruling-party leader, Dr. Sanjeeb, then leased back to Kalam for 10 years. Claiming administrative permission, Kalam tried to start pond digging—without any legal approval, according to the Puthia Upazila Nirbahi Officer (UNO).

In response, locals united and drove out the excavation team, vandalizing the illegal machinery and seizing locally made weapons found at the site.

Police were deployed the following morning to prevent further escalation, and officials confirmed that no permission had been granted for such excavation. Despite Kalam’s claims of legality, the incident is being widely seen as a coordinated attempt to encroach upon minority-owned agricultural land under political cover.

This case reflects a growing pattern of intimidation and land seizures targeting Hindu communities in rural Bangladesh. Local residents are demanding legal action, land restoration, and protection from politically backed aggression.

6. Hindu Family in Chandpur’s Haimchar Faces Continuous Land Grabbing, Threats to Life, and Forced Eviction Attempts by Local Musclemen
Date of Incident:
May 24, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chandpur
Upazilla: Haimchar
Details
In a desperate appeal for safety and justice, the only Hindu family in Mahajompur village, Ward No. 8, 2 No. Algir Durgapur North Union of Haimchar upazila, Chandpur district, held a press conference at Chandpur Press Club on Saturday afternoon. Members of the victimized family — Gopal Krishna Majhi, Molina Das, Amulya Majhi, Jugal Majhi, and Debjani Sarkar — described a relentless campaign of intimidation, land grabbing, and communal harassment by influential local land grabbers from neighboring Muslim-majority villages.

According to their written statement, a group of influential individuals — Abdus Salam Talukdar, Abdur Rahim Talukdar, Abdur Kader Talukdar from Talukdar Bari, and Abdus Sattar Gazi, Anwar Ullah Gazi, Shahid Ullah Gazi, Masud Gazi, Hannan Gazi, Mannan Gazi from Gazi Bari — have been illegally claiming the Hindu family’s ancestral and purchased property for years. These men, described as land grabbers and local thugs, have already seized two ponds, orchards, and several plots of land. Despite a local arbitration that confirmed the family’s ownership of at least three acres of land currently under illegal occupation, the accused escalated their threats and violence.

On May 19, 2025, when the family attempted to clear trees on their own land to build a house, the attackers intervened, even bringing police to halt the work. The next day, May 20, the accused obtained a temporary court injunction from the Additional District Magistrate’s Court to prevent any construction on the family’s property, further crippling their ability to defend their land. Since then, the family has been subjected to continuous death threats, warnings of arson attacks on their home, and harassment through false police cases designed to drive them out.

Fearing for their lives, the family has repeatedly appealed to authorities but found no effective protection. They reported that locals who tried to support them — such as village elder Tajul Islam Gazi — were also made defendants in false cases, discouraging others from helping. The family is now publicly urging the Home Affairs Adviser, the Home Secretary, the District Commissioner, and the Police Superintendent to ensure their safety, protect their property from illegal seizure, and hold the perpetrators accountable.

7. Hindu Villagers in Bogura Deceived in Loan Scam Disguised as Government Program
Date of Incident:
May 22, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Bogura
Upazilla: Nandigram
Details
In what appears to be a targeted financial fraud affecting mainly Hindu villagers, more than 160 people from the Hindu-majority villages of Hatuya, Alaipur, and Gundai in Bogura’s Nandigram upazila have accused local government officials of embezzling 1.4 million taka under the false promise of government loans.

The victims allege that Abdur Rauf, the Upazila Youth Development Officer, his assistant Ibrahim Khalil, and one Raju Ahmed of Kundarhat collectively defrauded them. The accused claimed they were distributing loans of Tk 1 lakh each for cattle fattening under a government scheme. Each villager was asked to deposit Tk 6,000–9,000 as "savings" to qualify for the scheme. Trusting the officials and their authority, the villagers handed over a total of Tk 14 lakh over several months.

Nearly nine months have passed since the deposits were made, but no loan has been disbursed. Repeated promises and false deadlines followed, but the victims—many of whom belong to economically struggling Hindu farming families—received nothing in return.

On May 20, the victims submitted a written complaint to the Nandigram Upazila Nirbahi Officer (UNO), Laila Anjuman Banu, seeking justice and immediate return of their hard-earned money. The following day, they staged a peaceful sit-in protest in front of the Youth Development Office, demanding action. Police later dispersed the gathering, reportedly assuring the villagers of a refund—an assurance yet to materialize.

Juthi Rani, a resident of Gundai and one of the victims, stated that the youth development officer himself had directed them to deposit the money with Raju Ahmed. "We trusted the government officers. Now neither our savings nor the promised loan is in sight. We feel helpless and deceived," she said.

When questioned, Officer Abdur Rauf denied personal involvement, blaming Raju Ahmed for collecting the money under false pretenses by misusing the office's name. The UNO has reportedly ordered an investigation, and initial steps have been taken against Raju. However, the role of government officials remains under scrutiny as Hindu villagers continue to demand accountability.

This incident highlights how even government-linked schemes can be weaponized to exploit minority communities under the veil of bureaucracy and trust. The victims, primarily from the Hindu community, now seek legal redress and recovery of their stolen money, fearing this may not be an isolated case of communal financial exploitation.

Details
In a disturbing act of suspected communal intimidation, the ancestral home of a Hindu family in Raghunathpur village, under Kaliganj upazila of Jhenaidah district, was deliberately set ablaze by unidentified miscreants during the midnight hours of Thursday, 23 May 2024. The attack follows a series of prior harassment incidents, including stone pelting, smear attacks with human excreta, and nighttime intimidation — all aimed at terrorizing the Hindu Haldar family.

The fire broke out around 11:30 PM and continued until 1:00 AM, completely burning haystacks, stored firewood, and the cowshed located within the compound of Nirmal Haldar’s family home. The total damage is estimated to be over 4 lakh Bangladeshi taka (~$3,500 USD). The fire was brought under control only with the help of local residents and the Kaliganj Fire Service.

Family members believe this was a deliberate communal act intended to spread fear. Notably, this attack occurred after repeated earlier incidents, where unknown perpetrators:

Smeared feces on kitchen windows

Pelted the tin-roofed house and upper floors with bricks

Threatened the family with psychological and material harm

Despite these incidents, no effective action was taken, and Thursday night’s fire marks the most violent escalation.

The victims include Prashanta Haldar, a well-known dramatist and founder of the Dhaka-based theater group Anuswar, and his sister Shraboni Rakhi, a journalist. Upon hearing the news, they rushed from Dhaka to be with their aging father and uncle, who live in the home along with a few women and a younger sibling.

According to Prashanta Haldar:

“The fire began behind our two-story home, in a shed where hay and fuelwood were kept. It quickly spread to the cowshed and storage area. If the flames had reached the main house, it could’ve taken lives. We’re devastated, and the motive is clearly malicious.”

“This is not an accident. Someone did this with the clear intention to harm or frighten us. We’ve sought legal help.”

Despite the mounting evidence of targeted abuse, the police have not yet arrested any suspects. Locals remain tight-lipped, possibly out of fear or complicity.

Kaliganj Police OC Shahidul Islam Hawlader confirmed:

“We have received a complaint and are investigating the matter. Legal action will follow based on findings.”

This incident adds to the growing list of intimidation and violence faced by religious minorities, particularly Hindus, in rural Bangladesh, where perpetrators often go unpunished and victims live in fear and silence.

9. Communal Attack on Hindu Matua Village in Jessore: 18 Homes Burned Amid Religious Festival
Date of Incident:
May 21, 2025
Division: Khulna
District: Jashore
Upazilla: Abhaynagar
Specific Location: Barenda Para
Details
On the evening of Thursday, in the Hindu Matua-majority village of Barada Para in Dhoromoshihati, under Abhaynagar Upazila in Jessore, a brutal communal attack left 18 homes burned, ransacked, and looted while a peaceful religious festival was underway. The perpetrators, reportedly supporters of a slain local political leader, unleashed violence targeting the marginalized Hindu community, causing immense fear and displacement.

The village, situated deep within the waterlogged Bhobdoh region, was observing its annual Matua yajna, a sacred ritual gathering with food arranged for over 500 attendees. During the festival, local BNP-affiliated Krishak Dal (Farmers’ Wing) leader Tariqul Islam Sardar was killed nearby under disputed circumstances linked to a land and fishery conflict.

Instead of targeting those responsible, a mob — allegedly supporters of the deceased politician — took brutal revenge on the entire Hindu settlement. Within minutes of the murder, the mob stormed the village, attacking homes, women, and elders. Cooking pots were overturned, worship interrupted, and sacred spaces desecrated. The mob beat attendees, destroyed religious offerings, and set fire to houses — even as women and children screamed in terror.

Many homes burned for nearly four hours before fire services and police arrived. Residents claim authorities were either delayed or deliberately blocked by attackers, as mobs continued their rampage unchecked.

Elderly victims like former school principal Bikash Chandra Biswas, whose home was among those torched, spoke of helplessness:

“My old mother begged them to take everything but not burn the house. They threatened to burn her alive too.”

Another victim, Manab Biswas, recounted:

“They took over a lakh in cash, gold chains, smashed our TV, fridge, even broke the dressing table and then set fire to everything. They beat the women and guests who came for the yajna.”

Despite the scale of destruction, no arrests have been made. Three days have passed without a single case being filed by the police or the family of the slain politician. Victims, meanwhile, are living in constant fear, seeking refuge at relatives’ homes and avoiding their village after sundown. Many say the trauma and threat of renewed violence haunt them.

While local police downplay the incident as a "non-political" result of a fishery business dispute, community leaders and human rights observers argue the attack bore clear communal intent, given it targeted a Hindu religious gathering unrelated to the murder.

Local journalist Sajed Rahman told BBC Bangla:

“The killing was used as a pretext. Hundreds attacked a peaceful Hindu religious ceremony. This was a communal reprisal.”

Though token relief (tin sheets, rice, and small cash aid) was distributed, the state’s failure to provide legal redress, arrests, or protection has left the Hindu community abandoned and traumatized.

This incident is part of a worrying pattern of communal violence against Bangladesh’s Hindu minorities in recent months. As political tensions rise, vulnerable religious communities are again becoming scapegoats, raising urgent concerns for human rights and rule of law in the country.
