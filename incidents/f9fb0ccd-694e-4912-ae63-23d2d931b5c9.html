1. Hindu Couple Brutally Attacked and Killed in Cumilla; Allegations of Communal Motive and Cover-Up
Date of Incident:
December 25, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Comilla
Upazilla: Debidwar
Details
In a horrific act of communal violence, an elderly Hindu couple—Nitai Bhowmik and his wife—were brutally attacked and killed in their home in Barkamta village, Debidwar Upazila, Cumilla District, on the night of December 26, 2024, at around 8:15 PM.

The attackers, reportedly led by Md. Titu Swarnakar, son of Md. Yusuf Swarnakar, were part of a local Muslim gang that allegedly stormed the couple’s residence with knives and sticks, aiming to kill and loot their property. Both victims died on the spot due to the severe nature of the assault.

Shockingly, both the victims and the perpetrators are from the same village, yet instead of legal action being pursued, local BNP leaders are allegedly attempting to suppress the case through informal arbitration (salish)—a move widely condemned by human rights observers as an attempt to deny justice and protect the attackers.

Community members fear the assault was motivated not just by greed, but by religious hatred, as Hindu families in rural Bangladesh increasingly face targeted violence and systemic marginalization. Calls for justice have intensified as the family and locals demand criminal prosecution and government intervention, warning against any political protection or backdoor settlements.

This attack adds to the growing list of violent crimes against minorities that are often buried under silence, social pressure, or political influence—leaving victims without justice and communities in fear.
