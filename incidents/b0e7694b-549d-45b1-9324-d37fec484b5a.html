69. Hindu Woman UP Member Basana Mallik (45) Allegedly Gang-Raped, Poisoned to Death in Narail Sadar – Family Cries for Justice
Date of Incident:
December 23, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Narail
Police Station: Narail
Specific Location: Daulatpur, Maizpara Union, Narail Sadar
Details
In a horrifying act of gendered and communal violence, Basana Mallik (45), a Hindu woman and elected Union Parishad member of Maijpara Union (Wards 1, 2, and 3) in Narail Sadar Upazila, was allegedly gang-raped by a group of men and later poisoned to death. The incident has sent shockwaves through the local community, with widespread calls for justice.

According to the victim's family, Basana Mallik was lured to Daulatpur Bazar on Tuesday, after receiving a phone call from a local youth named Rajibul, asking her to collect some dues. She later visited a female acquaintance’s house in Daulatpur village, where several men—Faruk Mollah, Chanchal, Shafiqul, and Kibria—arrived, questioned her, and after Rajibul fled the scene, forcibly dragged her into a nearby orchard.

There, she was brutally gang-raped, and the assailants allegedly recorded the assault. They then threatened to release the video and demanded two lakh taka from her. When she resisted, they forcefully poured poison into her mouth, leaving her gravely ill.

Her husband reported that she returned home vomiting uncontrollably and eventually lost the ability to speak. She was rushed to Jessore Sadar Hospital, where she succumbed to her injuries on Thursday night. Before her death, she reportedly disclosed the names of her attackers to her only son.

One suspect, Faruk Mollah, has been arrested in Magura, while police say others will be detained soon. A rape and murder case is being prepared, and the police are treating the allegations with seriousness, pending forensic and postmortem results.

Local union members, including Akhtar Mollah and Piyari Begum, confirmed that community members widely believe Basana was raped and murdered. Villagers are demanding a fair investigation and exemplary punishment for the perpetrators.

This tragic case reflects a disturbing pattern of violence against women—especially from marginalized Hindu communities—facing threats both as public representatives and religious minorities. The community now awaits action not only from law enforcement, but from the nation’s conscience.
