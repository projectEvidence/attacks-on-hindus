16. Muslim goon Nayan Gazi (নয়ন গাজী) severely beats a Hindu man Shambhu Sarkar (শম্ভু সরকার) and his elderly father Nani Gopal Sarkar (ননী গোপাল সরকার) for not giving extortion money in Nesarabad (নেছারাবাদ), Pirojpur on March 09, 2025.
Date of Incident:
March 9, 2025
Division: Barishal
District: Pirojpur
Upazilla: Nesarabad
Specific Location: Samudaykathi union
Details
An old man and his son were beaten up in Nesarabad, Pirojpur. They alleged that Nayan Gazi (38) had demanded extortion money of 5 lakh taka from them. Nayan beat them up severely when they did not give him the money.

The incident took place on Sunday afternoon in Maishani village of Samudaykathi union of the upazila.

The injured persons are Nani Gopal Sarkar (60) and his son Shambhu Sarkar (44). Among them, the seriously injured Shambhu Sarkar has been admitted to the upazila health complex.

Lima Akhter, a doctor from the emergency department of the health complex, said, "The condition of the injured Shambhu Sarkar is serious. He has severe injuries to his head and various parts of his body. He has been admitted to the hospital."

Nani Gopal Sarkar's daughter Khuku Rani Das said, "Nayan Gazi has been demanding 5 lakh taka from my father for a few days. But I did not tell anyone about the matter until now out of fear. Apart from that, he was pressuring us to write off our land to him as we did not have any cash. This (Sunday) morning, he also pressured us to register the land in his name or give the money several times. When we could not pay the money, he entered the house and beat my brother severely. Later, when he found my father on the street, he also beat him up. Nayan has been torturing us mentally and physically for a long time."

19. A 3-year-old child in Baralekha Upazila of Moulvibazar was raped by the shop owner Redwan Islam Arif when the child was buying candy from a shop that is downstairs from their residence.
Date of Incident:
March 2, 2025
Division: Sylhet
District: Moulvibazar
Upazilla: Barlekha
Police Station: Baralekha
Specific Location: মৌলভীবাজারের বড়লেখা উপজেলা
Details
The incident happened on the morning Monday, 03 March 2025, the child, who lives with her family at a rented house, was given Tk 30 to buy candy from a shop below their residence. When the child did not return home promptly, the mother went downstairs and found the shopkeeper undressing and assaulting the child. Upon witnessing the incident, the mother screamed and rescued the child.

The victim's mother filed a case with the Barlekha Police Station last night, and police subsequently arrested the accused — the 17-year-old shopkeeper — in connection to the case, said Abul Kashem Sarkar, OC of Barlekha Police Station.

Officer-in-charge of Baralekha Police Station Abul Kashem Sarkar said that the child was first taken to Baralekha Upazila Health Complex and later transferred to Sylhet MAG Osmani Medical College Hospital for advanced treatment.

The child's physical condition became more complicated as he was suffering from thalassemia. The child is currently undergoing treatment at a private hospital in Sylhet.

The child's mother said that she has been suffering from insecurity since the incident. She said, "The owner of the house is threatening me because I told the police about the evidence of torture. On the day of the incident, they did not even let the child go to the hospital."

21. Islamic militants of Hizb-ut-Tahrir threaten to burn community centers if musical instruments are played at Hindu weddings
Date of Incident:
February 28, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Specific Location: Various Community Centers in Chittagong City
Details
In the wake of the threat from the banned militant organization Hizb-ut-Tahrir, complications have arisen regarding the community centers in Chittagong city where weddings of traditional religious followers were booked. The militant organization has threatened through a handwritten letter that no musical instruments will be used at weddings of traditional religious followers. If this is not agreed to, the community centers will be set on fire.

It is worth mentioning that in late January, all community centers around the parade ground in Chittagong were forced to cancel advance bookings for Hindu, Buddhist and non-Muslim weddings on the occasion of the waz mahfil of Jamaat-e-Islami speaker Mizanur Rahman Azhari. As a result, those families who had already printed wedding cards and sent out invitations were affected.

Many were forced to change the wedding date, and many were forced to book the ceremony at a far-off community center at a much higher rent. They were financially affected and harassed.

Meanwhile, there has been widespread excitement across the city over the threat of Hizb ut Tahrir. However, no statement has been received from any responsible person from the police or administration regarding this matter.

Date of Incident:
February 20, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Rajbari
Upazilla: Baliakandi
Details
Six days after going missing, a man's throat was slit in the Gorai River in Baliakandi Upazila of Rajbari. Police recovered the body from the Gorai River in Pushamla village of the upazila on Thursday afternoon. He had gone missing at the local jungle market on February 14.

The name of the recovered body is Utpal Kumar Biswas (45). He is the son of Birendra Nath Biswas of Pushamla village in Jungle Union of Baliakandi Upazila. He was a farmer.

Some locals said that after seeing the throat slit body of an unidentified man stuck in the Magura side of the Gorai River in Pushamla village, the locals informed the Baliakandi Police Station and the Sreepur Police Station of the neighboring Magura. Since it was in Baliakandi, the Baliakandi Police Station and the Faridpur Naval Police arrived and recovered the body. According to family and police sources, Utpal Kumar Biswas left his house on February 14 to watch the Mahanama Yajna at the local jungle market. When he did not return home at night, his family members started searching for him. On February 15, Utpal Kumar Biswas' elder brother Gautam Kumar Biswas filed a general diary (GD) at Baliakandi Police Station.

Baliakandi Police Station Officer-in-Charge (OC) Md. Jamal Uddin told Prothom Alo that Utpal Kumar Biswas used to work in agriculture. He is alleged to be a regional leader of the banned extremist Sarbahara Dal and a member of Shishir Bahini. However, there is no case against him at the police station. It is believed that professional killers killed him. After the body was examined, it was sent to the Rajbari Sadar Hospital morgue for an autopsy. Preparations are underway for a murder case in this incident at the police station.

30. Hindu woman singled out and gang raped in the back of a moving bus en route to Rajshahi while robbery. Although witnesses confirmed, Police initially denied rape.
Date of Incident:
February 17, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Natore
Upazilla: Baraigram
Details
The bus, which was robbed at midnight on the Dhaka-Rajshahi route, was occupied by robbers for about three and a half hours. During this time, female passengers were also molested and raped, the passengers on the bus alleged.

Based on the passengers' complaints, the Natore police have arrested the driver, the driver's assistant and the supervisor of the bus and handed them over to the court. It is learnt that they have been released on bail from there.

BBC Bangla spoke to two of the passengers who were on the bus last Monday (February 17) night. They allege that at least one person was raped on the bus that night.

Since the bus was in motion, the names of multiple places are emerging in this incident. Notable among them are Baraigram in Natore and Mirzapur police stations in Tangail.

Testimonies of passengers:

22-year-old Sohag Hasan is a businessman. His home is in Baraigram. Omar Ali from this village and he had gone to Dhaka together on business.

That day, when it was late to finish work, they hurriedly boarded the bus.

He said that even though the car was loaded with passengers, the driver and his accomplices put seven or eight more people in the car halfway and then one of them sat in the driver's seat.

The rest of the group then approached the passengers and held knives to their throats. They had forbidden them to turn on the bus lights, he said.

"And the biggest thing is, they were standing in front of everyone with knives. They even stabbed five or six people. Out of fear, no one said anything. Everyone had their heads down. They were saying, 'Keep your eyes closed. If you look, I will cut your eyes off,'" he added.

Sohag Hasan and Omar Ali had 120,000 taka. When the robbers asked, they initially gave 20,000 taka. But the driver and his assistants "showed" the robbers that they had more money, Mr. Hasan claimed.

"Because I didn't want to pay, they threw me down and held me on my chest until I paid. And they kept me down until I got off the bus."

At that time, they couldn't speak, couldn't raise their heads, couldn't open their eyes. All they could hear were the screams and cries of two female passengers on the bus.

"Their seats were in the middle of the bus. There was a Hindu in between them, and her husband was with her," said Mr. Hasan.

The robbers first took everything the woman had.

"Then, right in front of us, a boy dragged the woman to the back seat and forcibly took her away. When her husband tried to stop her, he beat her up a lot," said Sohag Hasan.

"They also raped her. When they took her back, she was screaming a lot. They wouldn't let us go. We could only hear the screams. She was crying loudly. But there was nothing we could do there."

Pausing, he added, "No one would scream like that if they hadn't raped her."

Majnu Akand, a 73-year-old jaggery trader, was also on the bus that day. He also said, "The screaming we heard that night... the respect of our mother and sister... we had no words inside the car. They were hurting their dignity, they were fighting."

He also said that two women were the most tortured. One was about 20 years old, the other 25-30 years old.

Stating that "I don't know whether the two women complained of rape or not" at the police station, he said, "We know that two people were tortured inside the car."

Regarding the second woman being talked about, Sohag Hasan said, "Another woman, probably 25-30 years old. She took everything. She was two seats in front of us. They wouldn't let us look. She was touching different parts of her body. When we were going to protest again and again, they tried to beat us. They took only the Hindu girl in the back of the bus and sexually assaulted her".

Police denies rape:
Meanwhile, passengers say that at least one rape incident occurred on the bus that night. But the OCs of Baraigram and Mirzapur, both police stations, say they are not aware of any such incident.

Baraigram OC Sirajul Islam told the BBC, "I have not found any information about rape. No one has told me about any such incident or I have not found any witnesses."

And Mr. Islam was quoted by the Mirzapur OC as saying, "Many people are talking about rape. But I do not know anything about it. The Baraigram OC said that nothing like this happened."
