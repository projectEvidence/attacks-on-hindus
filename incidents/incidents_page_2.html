Specific Location: Lakirpara, Kushla Union (লাকিরপাড়া, কুশলা ইউনিয়ন)
Details
A robbery incident took place in the afternoon at the house of a man named Paul Majumder Khokon in Kotalipara, Gopalganj. At that time, the robbers tied Paul Majumder Khokon's son Piyas Majumder (22) and killed him, and looted cash and gold ornaments.

At that time, people around rushed to take Piyas Majumder to the Kotalipara Upazila Health Center, where the doctor on duty declared him dead.

17. Robbers hacked Hindu gold trader Dilip Das to death in front of his wife and looted about 25 bhori of gold in Savar on March 09, 2025 as they were about to close their shop
Date of Incident:
March 9, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Dhaka
Upazilla: Savar
Specific Location: Nayarhat Bazaar Swarnapotti area of ​​Ashulia
Details
Robbers hacked Hindu gold trader Dilip Das to death in front of his wife and looted about 25 bhori of gold in Savar as they were about to close their shop.

The incident happened around 9:00pm at Nayarhat Bazaar Swarnapotti, known for its numerous gold jewellery shops. According to witnesses, three armed men arrived in a white car and detonated crude bombs to create panic. They then approached Dilip, who was about to close his shop, and stabbed him indiscriminately before snatching the bag of gold he was carrying. His wife was with him at the time. The criminals detonated more crude bombs before fleeing the scene in their vehicle. Dilip was rushed to Enam Medical College Hospital in critical condition, but he succumbed to his injuries during treatment.

His wife, Saraswati Das, said, "The armed men hacked my husband to death and looted 20 to 25 bhori of gold in his bag right in front of me."

Dr Merajur Rayhan, emergency medical officer at Enam Medical College Hospital, told The Daily Star that Dilip had suffered severe injuries to his chest, face, and multiple parts of his body. "We started treatment immediately, but he died due to excessive bleeding," he said.

24. Islamic militants of Hizb-ut-Tahrir threaten to burn community centers if musical instruments are played at Hindu weddings
Date of Incident:
February 28, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Specific Location: Various Community Centers in Chittagong City
Details
In the wake of the threat from the banned militant organization Hizb-ut-Tahrir, complications have arisen regarding the community centers in Chittagong city where weddings of traditional religious followers were booked. The militant organization has threatened through a handwritten letter that no musical instruments will be used at weddings of traditional religious followers. If this is not agreed to, the community centers will be set on fire.

It is worth mentioning that in late January, all community centers around the parade ground in Chittagong were forced to cancel advance bookings for Hindu, Buddhist and non-Muslim weddings on the occasion of the waz mahfil of Jamaat-e-Islami speaker Mizanur Rahman Azhari. As a result, those families who had already printed wedding cards and sent out invitations were affected.

Many were forced to change the wedding date, and many were forced to book the ceremony at a far-off community center at a much higher rent. They were financially affected and harassed.

Meanwhile, there has been widespread excitement across the city over the threat of Hizb ut Tahrir. However, no statement has been received from any responsible person from the police or administration regarding this matter.
