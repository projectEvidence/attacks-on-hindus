16. Hindus were served beef in a public Iftar gathering by Muslim organisers although organisers knew there would be Hindu guests.
Date of Incident:
March 21, 2025
Division: Khulna
District: Jashore
Upazilla: Jessore Sadar
Specific Location: Bangladesh Computer Samity Jessore branch at Case D Light Restaurant, JS Tower, Jessore
Details
There have been allegations of Hindus being served beef Tehari at an Iftar party organized by Bangladesh Computer Samity, Jessore. This has angered Hindu members of the association. Many have termed the incident as “religious hatred”.
Such allegations were made at an Iftar party organized by Bangladesh Computer Samity Jessore branch at Case D Light Restaurant, JS Tower, Jessore on Saturday evening, March 22.

Founder Chairman of Jessore branch Apu Saha said in a Facebook post at 12:14 pm on March 22, that I was the founder chairman of Jessore branch and elected chairman 3 times in a row. Also, many people after me have performed important duties in this organization with good reputation. Like every year, this year too, the association has organized an Iftar party. But the example set by the new leaders by causing such a hateful incident has brought the image of this organization to the bottom.

He further said, for those of us who do computer business, the emotions towards this organization are on another level. Today is a shameful day in the history of Bangladesh Computer Association, Jessore branch. Today, beef Tehri was served at the Iftar party organized by the association. Out of the 58 members in the association, 22/23 are traditional religious people.

He further said, I knew that those with whom I spent most of my life had competition in their minds about business, which is normal. However, I did not understand the hatred they had towards us.

Former chairman of the association, Partha Pratim Debnath (Rati), admitted the truth of the incident and said, we, the traditionalists, started the Iftar mahfil in this computer association. Such an incident has not happened in any previous year. The matter is highly condemnable and we have strongly protested.

In this regard, the current chairman of the association, Faruk Jahangir Ali Khan Tipu, did not respond to call to his mobile phone. However, when other members protested, he admitted his mistake and said that the people from the food committee did this without informing them.

18. Illegal Demolition and Encroachment of Historic Hindu Temple in Haat-Hazari, Chattogram
Date of Incident:
March 19, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Upazilla: Hathazari
Specific Location: Kachari Road
Details
In the heart of Haat-Hazari town lies the centuries-old Sri Jagannath Dev Mandir, a sacred space for the local Sanatani (Hindu) community. This ancient temple had been a place of worship, pilgrimage, and community gathering for generations. Yet in a shocking display of communal betrayal and greed, the temple was illegally demolished on the night of May 30, 2024, under the cover of darkness.

Two individuals—Amal Krishna Choudhury and Ashok Choudhury, both identifying as Hindus—colluded with a Muslim land developer, Jahangir Alam, to execute this demolition. Posing as unauthorized caretakers, they signed an illegitimate agreement with “Super Properties” to bulldoze the temple and construct a commercial high-rise building on this holy land.

This action was taken despite an ongoing legal battle, where in 2015, the Chattogram District Judge Court had ruled in favor of the Sanatani community, recognizing their claim to the temple. The matter is currently pending in the Supreme Court. Regardless, the accused went ahead and violently razed the temple, destroying not only the religious structure but also sacred trees and the spiritual sanctity of the land.

Local Sanatani voices allege that village simplicity and internal betrayal made the temple vulnerable to such encroachment. Community members are outraged that even fellow Hindus—driven by greed and personal gain—could hand over temple property to outsiders, facilitating what they see as a communal land grab.

The plan is to build a commercial market, which could allow people of other faiths to establish their institutions in proximity to the temple, ultimately disrupting Hindu religious practices such as chanting, drums, and puja offerings. Locals fear that this is part of a larger communal design to erase minority religious heritage from Bangladesh’s public spaces.

Despite having complete documentation of ownership and temple history, the community’s repeated appeals to authorities have gone unanswered. This has left them feeling vulnerable, betrayed, and abandoned.

Community Appeal:
The Sanatani community of Chattogram urgently calls upon the Bangladesh government, local administration, and all justice-loving citizens to:

Stop the construction of any structure on the temple land immediately.

Investigate the illegal demolition and charge those responsible for contempt of court and communal provocation.

Restore the original temple and declare it a protected heritage site.

Ensure that no commercial or non-Hindu religious institutions are permitted to operate on this land.

This incident is a clear example of communal encroachment disguised as property development. It is an attack not only on a structure but on the cultural and religious identity of Bangladesh’s Hindu minority.

Nature of the Violation
Land Grabbing: By illegal sebāyets and collusion with a Muslim real estate company

Demolition: Partial destruction on 30 May 2024

Construction: Plan to build a commercial complex on temple land

Environmental Destruction: All trees within the premises cut down

Details
A mother of three (40) of Sanatan Dharma was raped in her house in Raipura, Narsingdi. The accused threatened to kill her husband and wife when the incident came to light. While leaving, they threatened to slaughter the couple if they told anyone about the incident.

Police said that accused Rakib is a drug dealer and user. He is an accused in multiple drug cases. Multiple cases are pending against him in different police stations.

On Monday afternoon, the victim himself appeared at the Narsingdi Superintendent of Police's office and narrated the incident to Additional Superintendent of Police (Administration and Finance) Md. Kalimullah. After hearing the incident, the Additional Superintendent of Police ordered the officer-in-charge of Raipura Police Station to take legal action.

In this regard, Raipura Police Station Officer-in-Charge Adil Mahmud said, "The Additional Superintendent of Police called and informed me about the matter.
