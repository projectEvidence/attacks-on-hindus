16. Hindu Woman in Dhaka Threatened with Rape and Murder to Force Withdrawal of Ongoing Rape Case
Date of Incident:
May 7, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Dhaka
Upazilla: Dhaka City
Details
In Dhaka, a Hindu (Sanatani) woman has filed a General Diary (GD No. 1845) at Ramna Police Station after receiving repeated threats of rape and murder from the accused in an ongoing rape case and his associates. The survivor alleged that Md. Mesbaul Islam Quayes — son of Nimar Ali and Hosne Ara Begum, from the Faridabad Residential Area near Sylhet Airport — along with his relatives Nadel Morshed (son of Tarab Uddin and Nurjahan), Md. Janu Mia, and Golam Arafat Chowdhury have been intimidating her in order to force her to withdraw her case.

The survivor’s complaint states that Mesbaul Islam Quayes is already the principal accused in a case under Section 9(1) of the Women and Children Repression Prevention Act 2000 (amended 2003) for raping her. That case is currently under trial in Women and Children Repression Prevention Tribunal-8 and is also pending hearing in the High Court. She further learned through newspapers that Quayes is an accused in at least eight other criminal cases. He is presently in Sylhet Jail, yet continues to direct threats through his family members and associates.

According to the GD, the accused Quayes, with the help of his uncle Janu Mia and brother-in-law Nadel Morshed, has been pressuring her for a settlement. On the 5th of this month, four unknown men arrived at the gate of her rented home, sent by these individuals, offering money and other inducements to drop the case. When she refused, Janu Mia and Nadel Morshed directly threatened her, saying they would rape her and kill her if she did not comply. The victim has expressed deep fear for her life and appealed to the authorities for immediate protection and strict legal action against the perpetrators.

17. Former Hindu Public Prosecutor Abducted from Dhaka Hospital, Beaten and Extorted for Ransom
Date of Incident:
May 5, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Dhaka
Upazilla: Dhaka City
Specific Location: Mugda Medical College Hospital
Details
In a shocking incident targeting a Hindu legal professional, former Public Prosecutor (PP) Dilip Kumar Mallik, 65, was abducted from the Mugda Medical College Hospital area in Dhaka on 6 May 2025. According to police, the abductors demanded 30 lakh taka in ransom from his family, subjected him to physical assault, and threatened to kill him if the ransom was not paid.

Dilip Kumar Mallik’s son, Dipto Mallik, filed a General Diary (GD) at Jatrabari Police Station, prompting an investigation led by the Wari Division Police under the direction of Deputy Commissioner Harun-or-Rashid. During the ordeal, Dilip’s wife managed to collect and hand over 1 lakh taka to the abductors in an attempt to secure his safety.

Through enhanced intelligence operations and the use of technology, police conducted a raid at 9 PM on Friday, 9 May, successfully rescuing the victim unharmed and arresting three members of the abduction gang. Law enforcement officials have confirmed that the case is being investigated with utmost priority, and all perpetrators involved in the abduction, assault, and extortion will be brought to justice.

This incident has left the Hindu legal community in Dhaka deeply concerned, as it underscores the vulnerability and targeted nature of attacks on Hindu professionals in Bangladesh.

18. Mob Attacks Hindu Family in Barishal’s Koloshkathi; Home Looted, Temple Desecrated, Women Assaulted
Date of Incident:
May 5, 2025
Division: Barishal
District: Barishal
Police Station: Bakerganj
Specific Location: House of Khokon Das
Details
In a violent land-grabbing attack on May 6, 2024, a Hindu family's home and private temple were attacked, looted, and vandalized in Ward No. 1 of Koloshkathi Union, Bakerganj Upazila, Barishal District. The home belonged to the family of Bidhan Kumar Dash, who had been peacefully residing there for over 60 years.

According to victim Bithika Rani Dash, a group of over 100 armed individuals, including women in burqas, stormed the residence under the leadership of Khokon Khan, Tutul Khan, and Monir Khan—all sons of the late Haji Hossain Khan of nearby South Sadish village. The mob forcibly entered the premises, assaulted the women present, and desecrated the family's private temple, throwing its remains into the river.

Earlier, on May 2, the same group had attempted to illegally place boundary markers on the property, ignoring the family’s objections. Although a police-mediated arbitration was scheduled for May 20, the attackers returned before the date with a large armed mob, defying legal process and escalating the violence.

A formal complaint was filed at Bakerganj Police Station by Pran Kumar Dash. In response, police arrested two attackers, Hasnat alias Tutul and Selim Khan, at the scene. However, most of the assailants remain free, and the family continues to live in fear.

This brazen attack, targeting both property and a place of worship, is being condemned as yet another example of systematic intimidation and communal aggression against religious minorities in rural Bangladesh. The victims are demanding justice, property protection, and accountability for all involved.

19. 48-Year-Old Nirmal Das’s Body Recovered from Sitakunda Coast; Sent for Autopsy"
Date of Incident:
May 4, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Upazilla: Sitakunda
Specific Location: Keora forest (Mangrove area), Guliakhali sea coast, Muradpur Union
Details
On May 5, 2025, a decomposed body was recovered from the Keora mangrove forest along the Guliakhali sea coast in Muradpur Union, Sitakunda Upazila, Chattogram. The body was later identified as Nirmal Dash (48), son of Rajendra Dash from Sonaichhari Union.

According to police and family sources, Nirmal had a habit of going to the sea for bathing, reportedly due to frequent burning sensations on his body. On May 1, he went for a sea bath but never returned home. His family searched extensively and later filed a missing person report with the local police .

That night, local fishermen spotted a body in the Keora forest while fishing and immediately called the national emergency hotline 999. A team from the Kumira River Police Outpost, led by Officer-in-Charge Wali Uddin, recovered the body and sent it to the Chattogram Medical College Hospital morgue for autopsy.

“We cannot confirm the cause of death at this stage,” said OC Wali Uddin. “Only after the postmortem will we be able to determine the details.”

The family and community are now awaiting the results of the investigation and have urged the authorities to ensure a fair and transparent inquiry into the circumstances surrounding Nirmal Dash’s mysterious death.

20. Communal Attack on Hindu RAB Member’s Family in Panchagarh: Home Looted and vandalised, Women Assaulted, Lives Threatened
Date of Incident:
May 1, 2025
Division: Rangpur
District: Panchagarh
Upazilla: Panchagarh Sadar
Details
On the morning of May 2, 2025, in a shocking communal attack in the Dangapara area of Panchagarh Sadar upazila’s Amarkhana Union, the home of a Hindu RAB member, Profulla Roy, was brutally attacked by a mob led by a man named Hamidul Islam. The assailants—an armed group of 20–25 individuals—stormed the residence of Ramesh Chandra Roy, a relative of Profulla, with machetes, rods, axes, iron tools, and bamboo sticks. They vandalized property, physically assaulted the family members, looted over two lakh taka in cash, gold jewelry, and valuable legal documents including land deeds. Women in the house were reportedly subjected to sexual harassment during the assault.

This violent incident, which has deeply traumatized the Hindu family, stems from a long-standing land dispute between Ramesh Chandra Roy and Hamidul Islam. However, the sheer scale, organization, and brutality of the attack point to a broader communal intent. The attackers acted with impunity, fully aware that the family belonged to a religious minority. Victim Ramesh Chandra Roy said, “They came with the clear intent to kill us. We no longer feel safe in our own land.”

Profulla Roy, a RAB member who was home on leave in preparation to join the 40 BGB unit, also alleged that he was deliberately targeted on the second day of his leave. “This was not a spontaneous fight—it was a calculated attack. I want justice,” he stated.

The victims have filed a case with Panchagarh Sadar Police Station (Case No. 30). Officer-in-Charge Md. Abdullahil Zaman confirmed that there had been two prior cases related to the land dispute. He added that police are currently conducting raids to arrest the accused. However, the Hindu community in the region remains fearful, calling for stronger protection and urgent legal action against those who orchestrated this communal violence.
