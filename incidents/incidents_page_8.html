106. Professor Dr. Kanta Roy Rimi could not attend work due to the protest of radical Islamic students at Naogaon Medical College
Date of Incident:
November 12, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Naogaon
Upazilla: Naogaon Sadar
Details
Professor Dr. Kanta Roy Rimi could not attend work due to the protest of radical Islamic students at Naogaon Medical College.
Several protesting students said that Dr. Kanta Roy used to teach at Dinajpur Medical College during the previous government. They alleged that she discriminated against students there in various ways. During the interview, Dr. Kanta Roy used to forbid students from wearing hijab.

It is known that on November 11, in a notification signed by Dur-re-Shahwaz, Deputy Secretary of the Health Education and Family Welfare Department, Professor Dr. Kanta Roy Rimi was posted to Naogaon Medical College. Then he came to the college around 9:30 am to join the college. After learning about the matter, students of different years of the medical college boycotted classes and started a movement to prevent his joining. The students raised various slogans in front of the academy building. After about 2 hours of movement, the students finally suspended the protest on the assurance of the principal. The students said that if he joins the college again in the future, they will call for a larger movement.

107. Idols were vandalized at the Sri Bhadra Kalimata Temple in Chatmohar upazila, Pabna, due to an alleged religiously offensive Facebook status
Date of Incident:
November 9, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Pabna
Upazilla: Chatmohar
Specific Location: Paschimpara, Handial union
Details
Three idols were vandalized at the Shri Bhadra Kalimata Temple in Paschimpara, Ballabhpur village, Handial union, Chatmohar upazilla, Pabna. The attack on the public Mahadev temple comes two months after the previous incident of attacks on Hindu homes and businesses.

Prabir Kumar Dutta Chaitanya, general secretary of Chatmohar Upazila Puja Udjavan Parishad, said that Prashant Kumar Saha (25), son of Kala Saha of Ballabhpur village in Handial union of the upazila, is a resident of Dhaka. According to Muslims, he posted a status on social media Facebook last Saturday (November 9) insulting their Holy Prophet. It is known that he lives in Dhaka. The muslim people of the area were enraged by this and took out protest processions that afternoon and night and warned Hindu businessmen not to open their businesses. As a result, the situation in the area remained tense throughout the day on Sunday (November 10). At one stage, the situation calmed down with the intervention of the administration. The businessmen were also allowed to open their shops. Meanwhile, around 8 pm on Sunday, several people took out a protest procession and attacked the Sarvajanin Mahadev Temple in Ballabhpur area and vandalized the idol. This spread panic among the Hindu community in the area. Later, the army, RAB and police members went to the spot and brought the situation under control.

November 7, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Chapai Nawabganj
Upazilla: Nawabganj Sadar
Specific Location: Shibtola
Details
On Friday, November 8, 2024, bricks were hurled at around 10 pm at the final moments of the Hindu Damodar Vrat ceremony at the Charjot Pratap Durgamata Thakurani Temple in Shivtala, Chapainawabganj Municipality area.

The tiled archway of the temple was damaged. The saints and devotees present in the temple were stranded for a long time.
Upon receiving the news, about a thousand and a half Hindu men and women from Charjot Pratap, Shibtala and surrounding neighborhoods and villages gathered and blocked the Chapainawabganj-Shibganj road. Police and army officials arrived at the roadblock that lasted for about an hour and a quarter from 10:30 pm. They warned of strict action if the people involved were not arrested within 24 hours. The roadblock was withdrawn following assurances from the law enforcement agencies.

Date of Incident:
November 4, 2024
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Details
Osman Ali, a businessman from Ali Store in Mia Shopping Center, Hazari Gali, Chittagong, shared a post on Facebook to ban ISKCON. In response, on Tuesday evening, November 5, angry people from the Hindu community surrounded his shop. During this, a joint force of police and army attacked Hindus. Later, the army conducted another raid in Hazari Gali until 11:30 pm and broke CCTV cameras indiscriminately. During the joint force's raid in that area, pictures of massive lathicharge and beatings were spread on social media. There have been allegations of oppression of people from the Hindu community over this incident. In particular, the issue of breaking the CCTV cameras of the joint force members during the raid has raised questions. Discussion and criticism are going on on social media. The remaining 33 people, who were under 15 and over 55 years of age and were not found to be involved in the incident, were said to have been released in the custody of their guardians. In this incident, Kotwali Police Station SI Mizanur Rahman filed a case on charges of obstructing government work, assaulting police and throwing acid. 49 people, including 500-600 unknown people, have been accused in the case. All of the 49 arrested are from the Hindu community.” The army sealed every shop in Hazari Gali that night. Three days later, when the army opened the CCTV hard disk of every shop, it was arrested.
Even Mana Das, who had gone to collect medicine for her wife, who was undergoing treatment for cancer at Chittagong Medical College, was arrested and sent to jail. Later, when the media published news about the incident under the headline "What will Mana's wife's ten-year-old son do now?", she was granted bail after 25 days.
