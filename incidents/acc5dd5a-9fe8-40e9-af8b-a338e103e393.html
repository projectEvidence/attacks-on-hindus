14. Jhenaidah – Only Hindu Family in Village Faces Land Grabbing, Threats, and Possible Eviction
Date of Incident:
May 11, 2025
Division: Khulna
District: Jhenaidah
Upazilla: Shailkupa
Details
In Sadhukhali village of Shailkupa upazila, Jhenaidah, the last remaining Hindu family in the village is facing attempts to forcibly grab their ancestral land. The family of Arun Biswas, who has lived on the land for generations, fears imminent eviction amid rising threats and intimidation.

Arun Biswas said, “We are still clinging to the land of our forefathers. All the other Hindu families from this village have left, but we stayed back out of attachment to our home. Now, with the harassment we are facing, I don’t think we can endure much longer.”

Local sources confirmed that the Biswas family has been living on their 27 decimal ancestral land for generations. In 1985, their Muslim neighbor Abdus Sattar falsely claimed ownership of the land and filed a court case. After a lengthy legal process, the court in 1990 cancelled Sattar’s deed. In 2011, Arun Biswas sought a permanent injunction, and in 2021, the court issued an order prohibiting Abdus Sattar from interfering with the property.

However, ignoring the court order, on Friday morning, Abdus Sattar and his associates attempted to illegally build a house on the land. When Arun Biswas’s four sons protested, they were met with threats and intimidation. Sattar’s men even managed to erect a small structure before leaving.

An elderly neighbor, Abdul Kader, testified: “I have seen Arun Biswas and his forefathers living here for the past 70 years. This attempt to take their land by force is deeply shameful.”

Arun’s son, Arbind Biswas, a rural medical practitioner, said: “We can’t even build our own house on our own land. Despite the court’s verdict in our favor, they are persecuting us. My parents are now forced to stay in the cattle shed.”

When contacted, Abdus Sattar’s son, Rezaul Islam, claimed they had legal ownership papers for the land, but could not produce any documents to support the claim.

Shailkupa Police Station Officer-in-Charge Masum Khan stated that they had not yet received a written complaint, but assured that necessary legal action would be taken if one was filed.

The Hindu family has appealed for urgent intervention to protect their home and ancestral property, fearing that without strong action, they will be the next victims of complete displacement from the village.
