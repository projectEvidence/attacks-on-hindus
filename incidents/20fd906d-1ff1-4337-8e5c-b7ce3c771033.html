219. Hindu households at Andharkota and Bollamukh village were attacked and looted by Islamist mob
Date of Incident:
August 4, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Jashore
Upazilla: Bagherpara
Specific Location: Andharkota village, Bollamukh village
Details
Several Hindu houses were attacked and looted at Andharkota village. Most of them were Hindu farmers or day labourer.
Golak Chandra Biswas, one of the many victims, claimed that the assailants had vandalized the family home that day. They also looted gold, 50,000 taka in cash, and a music box. They then took out a motorcycle and a bicycle that were kept on the balcony of the house and placed them on one side of the yard. They then took out blankets, pillows, and clothes from the house, placed them on the motorcycle and bicycle, and set them on fire.

    Notes: Father of Dipankar Biswas.

    Dipankar's father and former member of ward number 5 of Dhalgram Union Parishad, Kshitish Chandra Biswas (72), said that around 8 pm on August 5, about 150 people attacked his house with ramda, hansua, sticks, axes, iron rods and petrol-filled bottles. After vandalizing the house, they took four cows, 22 ducks, 12 chickens, 40 duck eggs, two sacks of rice and an electric motor. He and his wife were at home at the time of the attack. The miscreants demanded one lakh taka from them. If they did not give it, they threatened to pour petrol inside the house and set it on fire. He claims that he was saved by paying 50 thousand taka.
