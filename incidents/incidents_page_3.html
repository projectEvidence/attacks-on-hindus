31. A sixth-grade Hindu student was raped on her way home from school in Rangpur
Date of Incident:
March 17, 2025
Division: Rangpur
District: Rangpur
Upazilla: Mithapukur
Specific Location: বড় মির্জাপুর ইউনিয়ন/বড় হযরতপুর ইউনিয়নের চিথলী পশ্চিমপাড়া এলাকা
Details
A sixth-grade student was raped on her way home from private school in Mithapukur, Rangpur. The student's father has filed a case with the police station in this incident. The incident took place on Tuesday (March 18) afternoon. The police could not arrest the rapist.

According to police and local sources, the student, the daughter of a farmer from a village in Boro Mirzapur Union of the upazila, is studying in sixth grade at a local high school. The school is now closed. However, the girl used to go to school every day to study privately. She was returning home after finishing her private studies in the morning. Alam Mia (40), son of the deceased Abu Mia of the area, who was lying in wait in a corn field on the way, grabbed the girl and took her inside the corn field and raped her.

Later, when the girl heard her crying, people around the spot rescued her and admitted her to the hospital. The rapist fled. Mithapukur Police Station OC Abu Bakkar Siddique said that a case has been filed with the police station in this incident. The girl has been rescued and admitted to the hospital for a health check-up.

33. Bangladesh Medical University Associate Professor Dr. Anindita Dutta blocked and harassed on campus by Chhatra Dal leaders and activists and local mob
Date of Incident:
March 15, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Dhaka
Upazilla: Dhaka City
Police Station: Shahbag
Specific Location: PG Hospital
Details
Bangladesh Medical University (formerly BSMMU) Associate Professor Dr. Anindita Dutta was reportedly locked up on the campus. After a long 6 hours, she was rescued with the help of the university administration, army and police and sent home.

Dr. Anindita Dutta is the daughter of former MP and former Vice-Chancellor of Bangladesh Medical University, Professor Pran Gopal Dutta. She is an associate professor in the university's Radiology and Imaging Department. She is not known to have any political affiliation.

Investigations revealed that a group of people (some leaders of Shahbagh Thana Chhatra Dal and students) had locked up Anindita Dutta in her office in the Cancer Building in F Block of Bangladesh Medical University since Sunday (March 16) morning.

In this regard, Shahbagh Thana Chhatra Dal leader Rubel Ahmed said that there is a case against her for attacking the students before August 5. Based on the information of the plaintiff in the case, we came here and blocked her.

Bangladesh Medical University Proctor Professor Dr. Sheikh Farhad said that Pran Gopal sir's daughter works here. She has been detained here. We are also detained here with her. The army has also arrived at the scene.

University Vice-Chancellor Professor Dr. Shahinur Alam said, I heard that a group of students have come to the Cancer Building and taken up position. After informing the law enforcement agencies, the police came and took up position.

Shahbagh Police Station OC Khalid Mansur said, "So far, the Bangladesh Medical University authorities have not informed us anything about this. They have some internal rules and regulations. I don't know if there is any case against that person."

34. Two Hindu students, Bikarna Das Divya and Pranay Kundu, were expelled from the university for allegedly insulting Islam
Date of Incident:
March 15, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Pabna
Upazilla: Pabna Sadar
Details
The university administration has temporarily expelled two students of Pabna Science and Technology University on charges of insulting Islam. The names of the two students are Bikarna Das Divya and Pranay Kundu. They are studying in the Department of Urban and Regional Planning. The matter was learned from the university's proctor's office on Sunday (March 16).

University Proctor Professor Dr. Kamruzzaman Khan said, "Last Friday, allegations of insulting religion were made against two students of the Department of Urban and Regional Planning. Their department gave them a show cause notice. Although they responded to the show cause notice, it was reported that it was not satisfactory. An emergency meeting of the Disciplinary Board was held today on this issue.

In the meeting, it was decided to temporarily expel the two students from the Department of Urban and Regional Planning."

On Friday night (March 14), the Facebook posts and comments of Bikarna Das Divya and Pranay Kundu about insulting Islam went viral on social media. That night, general students protested on campus demanding the expulsion of the two students. Later, show cause notices were given to the two students.

The administration decided to temporarily expel the two students in an emergency disciplinary board meeting today.

Meanwhile, screenshots of the Messenger group of members of the Sanatan Vidyarthi Sangsad of the Pharmacy Department were leaked on social media last night. In that group, the students alleged that Bidyut Sarkar, Suborna Sarkar of the 2017-18 academic year, Dipu Biswas of the 2018-19 academic year, Tanoy Sarkar of the 2019-20 academic year, and Ankan Ghosh of the 2022-23 academic year had insulted Islam. The students started the protest at 11:30 am demanding the expulsion of these five new students.

37. Unknown Youth Attacked the temple at Raipur Mahamaya Mandir in Laxmipur
Date of Incident:
March 12, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Lakshmipur
Upazilla: Raipur
Details
An incident of deity vandalism has occurred at a temple in Raipur upazila of Lakshmipur. The incident took place around 9 PM on Thursday night at the Shri Shri Mahamaya Temple in Raipur municipality of the district. Following the incident, the army, police, and leaders of various political parties visited the site.

Rabindra Karmakar, the president of the temple committee, said that the deity of Goddess Saraswati was vandalized. The temple priest had gone to a nearby house to conduct a ritual. Jamilul Haque, Assistant Superintendent of Police (Raipur Circle), told Prothom Alo that the incident appears to be deliberate. Efforts are underway to identify the masked youth. Those involved in the incident will be identified and brought under the law.

Milon Mondal, General Secretary of the Lakshmipur District Hindu-Buddhist-Christian Unity Council, said the incident must be investigated promptly and those responsible brought to justice. He urged strong intervention from the administration on this matter.
