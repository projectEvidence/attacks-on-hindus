28. Looters stole cows and calves of the family of a Hindu widow, that was going through financial difficulties in Uttar Bhabanipur village of Juri, Moulvibazar
Date of Incident:
February 27, 2025
Division: Sylhet
District: Moulvibazar
Upazilla: Juri
Specific Location: Paschim Juri Union
Details
Showing a barn with a tin roof and bamboo fence in front of the house, Lucky said that due to financial difficulties, he sold an ox for 35,000 taka 10-15 days ago. There were two cows and two calves. After eating on the night of February 28, the whole family went to sleep. The next morning, March 1, he woke up and found the barn door unlocked. The cows were not inside. Saying this, Lucky burst into tears.

After the untimely death of her husband, Lucky Rani Dey, in addition to managing the family, was raising five cows. She planned to sell the cows to meet the family expenses and the education expenses of her two daughters. In need of money, she recently sold a cow. In the meantime, miscreants have broken the lock of the cowshed and stolen the remaining four cows. In this situation, Lucky has become destitute.

Lucky Rani is a resident of Uttar Bhabanipur village in Paschim Juri Union of Juri Upazila of Moulvibazar. Her husband Prashant Dey had a paan stall in the Bhabaniganj Bazar bus stand area of ​​the upazila's sadar. About three years ago, he suddenly died of a heart attack. The well-organized family was destroyed. After that, Lucky took over the family. Their elder daughter Riya Rani Dey will now appear for the SSC exam. The younger daughter Ruhi Rani Dey is studying in the fifth grade.
