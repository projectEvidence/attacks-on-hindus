381. Terror Attack on Kali Puja Mandap in Atrai: Worship Disrupted, Women Assaulted, Four Arrested
Date of Incident:
July 6, 2001
Division: Rajshahi
District: Naogaon
Upazilla: Atrai
Specific Location: Kali Puja Mandap
Details
Four individuals have been arrested in connection with a violent attack on a Hindu religious gathering in Dangapara village of Atrai upazila, which disrupted a Kali Puja celebration and left several worshippers injured.

According to eyewitnesses and police sources, the incident took place on the night of July 7, when members of the minority Hindu community were engaged in a Kali Puja at the local temple. A group of local miscreants allegedly approached Haripada Prang, president of the Dangapara Kali Mandir Puja Committee, demanding extortion money. When he refused, the attackers began assaulting him and others with punches and sticks, forcing the puja to come to a halt.

The attackers also reportedly vandalized nearby houses belonging to members of the minority community and issued death threats to Haripada Prang.

On July 8, Haripada Prang filed a case with Atrai Police Station, naming 12 individuals as accused. Police have since arrested four of the suspects, who have been sent to jail following preliminary legal proceedings.
