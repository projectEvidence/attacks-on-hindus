63. Planned Attack on Hindu Activist Akash Mistri's Home in Khulna: Family Members Assaulted, Property Looted and Destroyed
Date of Incident:
December 27, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Khulna
Police Station: Khulna
Details
On December 28, 2024, a premeditated attack was carried out on the residence of Akash Mistri, Khulna representative of the Bangladesh Sammilito Sanatani Hindu Jagaran Jote—an organization advocating for Hindu rights and awareness.

The attackers violently stormed the home, vandalized and looted property, and brutally assaulted family members, including Niranjan Chandra Mistri, Shobha Rani, Nayon Chandra Mistri, and elderly Nibha Rani (grandmother). The house was left in a devastated state, with valuables stolen and furnishings destroyed.

The clearly targeted nature of the assault, aimed at a visible Hindu rights advocate, has raised alarm among human rights observers and the local Hindu community. Many now fear that activism for religious minority rights is being met with violent retaliation.

Despite the severity of the incident, there has been no public assurance of justice or protection for the victimized family, prompting calls for urgent intervention by law enforcement and the government.

This attack underscores the increasing vulnerability of outspoken Hindu figures in Bangladesh, and the rising boldness of those who seek to silence them through terror and intimidation.
