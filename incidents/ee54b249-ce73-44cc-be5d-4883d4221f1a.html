132. BNP Leader Firoz Khan files a FALSE sedition case against Hindu Jagran Mancha leader Chinmoy Krishna Das Brahmachari and 19 others in a time fertile for minority persecution
Date of Incident:
October 30, 2024
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Details
On October 31, Firoz Khan, the general secretary of Chandgaon Mohra ward BNP in Chittagong city, filed a FALSE case against Chinmoy Krishna Das Brahmachari and 19 others at the Kotwali police station on charges of sedition. The case alleged that the national flag had been desecrated at the Independence Pillar at New Market intersection.

From https://www.somoynews.tv/news/2024-11-26/X1MJ2ZsP (attached pdf: https://drive.google.com/file/d/1OCZvDEZT_WOYwfhMgYM1mgBlklJRwK2U/view?usp=drive_link)
The case alleges that after the mass uprising in Bangladesh on August 5, the anti-discrimination student crowd hoisted a national flag on a pillar at Zero Point at the New Market intersection in the city; which is still there. On October 25, a grand rally was held at Laldighi ground under the initiative of Bangladesh Sanatan Jagran Mancha. Thousands of people from different areas of Chittagong division joined the rally. On that day, instigated by Chandan Kumar Dhar and nine other accused, the remaining accused hoisted the saffron-colored religious flag of the religious group ISKCON over the Zero Point pillar at New Market intersection and the national flag of Bangladesh nearby creating widespread anger among ordinary citizens.

Following this case, a statement by Chinmoy Krishna Das regarding the sedition case was released in a video message on the official Facebook page of ISKCON promoter Dham. In it, he called the case an attempt to divert the peaceful movement program of the Sanatanis to achieve their eight-point demands into a different sector. In the statement, Chinmoy Krishna Das said, "Our movement is to protest the attacks and torture carried out on the Sanatanis after August 5. This movement is not against the current government or the state."
