68. Extremists Attack Devotional Gathering at Tajhat Sheetala Temple in Rangpur During Kirtan Ceremony
Date of Incident:
December 22, 2024
Division: Rangpur
District: Rangpur
Upazilla: Rangpur Sadar
Specific Location: Sheetala Temple, Ranighat area
Details
In a shocking act of religious intolerance, a group of extremists attacked the Sheetala Mandir in Tajhat Ranighat, Rangpur District, during a peaceful “Naam Yajna” (devotional chanting) ceremony on a recent afternoon.

The local Sanatani Hindu community had voluntarily stopped using loudspeakers between 12:00 PM and 2:00 PM, out of respect for others in the neighborhood, to avoid any disturbance. Despite this goodwill gesture, extremists forcefully disrupted the religious event, raising serious concerns about the safety and rights of religious minorities in the region.

The attack occurred during a sacred Hindu ritual, where devotees were engaged in prayer and song inside the temple premises. The assailants' actions were unprovoked, and their motive appears to be rooted in communal hatred and an intent to intimidate a peaceful minority community.

Local Hindus are outraged and heartbroken, asking:

“We muted our own mics in respect of others—then why were we attacked during our prayer?”

Community leaders and residents are now demanding urgent legal action, protection of Hindu religious institutions, and clear government assurance that such acts of intolerance will not be tolerated. The incident highlights the fragile condition of religious freedom for minorities, where even silence and cooperation do not safeguard against hatred.
