Specific Location: মৌলভীবাজারের বড়লেখা উপজেলা
Details
The incident happened on the morning Monday, 03 March 2025, the child, who lives with her family at a rented house, was given Tk 30 to buy candy from a shop below their residence. When the child did not return home promptly, the mother went downstairs and found the shopkeeper undressing and assaulting the child. Upon witnessing the incident, the mother screamed and rescued the child.

The child's physical condition became more complicated as he was suffering from thalassemia. The child is currently undergoing treatment at a private hospital in Sylhet.

The child's mother said that she has been suffering from insecurity since the incident. She said, "The owner of the house is threatening me because I told the police about the evidence of torture. On the day of the incident, they did not even let the child go to the hospital."

51. Saraswati Idol Vandalized in Sirajganj’s Kazipur
Date of Incident:
February 28, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Sirajganj
Upazilla: Kazipur
Specific Location: Sonamukhi Bazar
Details
Miscreants vandalized a Saraswati idol at a temple in Sonamukhi of Kazipur upazila in Sirajganj.

The incident took place at the *Shikha Smriti Sarvajanin Durga Temple* in Sonamukhi Bazar of the upazila late on Saturday night (February 1), said Jatan Kumar Karmakar, the temple's caretaker.

Upon receiving the news, Kazipur Upazila Executive Officer (UNO) Dewan Akramul Haque and Kazipur Police Station Officer-in-Charge (OC) Nur Alam visited the scene on Sunday morning. Later in the afternoon, Deputy Commissioner Nazrul Islam and Superintendent of Police Faruk Ahmed also visited the site.

Temple caretaker Jatan Kumar Karmakar said that while offering puja at the temple on Sunday morning, the idol was found lying on the floor in a broken state. There is a stainless steel fence in front of the brick temple building. Someone from outside broke the idol inside the temple using a bamboo stick and threw it on the floor. The matter has been reported to the administration. He has demanded punishment for those involved in the incident.

Locals, who did not want to be named, said that a sinister group may have committed this act with the aim of creating unrest in society.

Kazipur Police Station OC Nur Alam said the scene has been inspected, and efforts are underway to identify those involved in the idol vandalism.

52. Islamic militants of Hizb-ut-Tahrir threaten to burn community centers if musical instruments are played at Hindu weddings
Date of Incident:
February 27, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Specific Location: Various Community Centers in Chittagong City
Details
In the wake of the threat from the banned militant organization Hizb-ut-Tahrir, complications have arisen regarding the community centers in Chittagong city where weddings of traditional religious followers were booked. The militant organization has threatened through a handwritten letter that no musical instruments will be used at weddings of traditional religious followers. If this is not agreed to, the community centers will be set on fire.

It is worth mentioning that in late January, all community centers around the parade ground in Chittagong were forced to cancel advance bookings for Hindu, Buddhist and non-Muslim weddings on the occasion of the waz mahfil of Jamaat-e-Islami speaker Mizanur Rahman Azhari. As a result, those families who had already printed wedding cards and sent out invitations were affected.

Many were forced to change the wedding date, and many were forced to book the ceremony at a far-off community center at a much higher rent. They were financially affected and harassed.

Meanwhile, there has been widespread excitement across the city over the threat of Hizb ut Tahrir. However, no statement has been received from any responsible person from the police or administration regarding this matter.
