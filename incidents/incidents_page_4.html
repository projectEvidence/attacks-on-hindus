46. Hindu woman singled out and gang raped in the back of a moving bus en route to Rajshahi while robbery. Although witnesses confirmed, Police initially denied rape.
Date of Incident:
February 16, 2025
Division: Rajshahi
District: Natore
Upazilla: Baraigram
Details
The bus, which was robbed at midnight on the Dhaka-Rajshahi route, was occupied by robbers for about three and a half hours. During this time, female passengers were also molested and raped, the passengers on the bus alleged.

Based on the passengers' complaints, the Natore police have arrested the driver, the driver's assistant and the supervisor of the bus and handed them over to the court. It is learnt that they have been released on bail from there.

BBC Bangla spoke to two of the passengers who were on the bus last Monday (February 17) night. They allege that at least one person was raped on the bus that night.

Since the bus was in motion, the names of multiple places are emerging in this incident. Notable among them are Baraigram in Natore and Mirzapur police stations in Tangail.

Testimonies of passengers:

22-year-old Sohag Hasan is a businessman. His home is in Baraigram. Omar Ali from this village and he had gone to Dhaka together on business.

That day, when it was late to finish work, they hurriedly boarded the bus.

He said that even though the car was loaded with passengers, the driver and his accomplices put seven or eight more people in the car halfway and then one of them sat in the driver's seat.

The rest of the group then approached the passengers and held knives to their throats. They had forbidden them to turn on the bus lights, he said.

"And the biggest thing is, they were standing in front of everyone with knives. They even stabbed five or six people. Out of fear, no one said anything. Everyone had their heads down. They were saying, 'Keep your eyes closed. If you look, I will cut your eyes off,'" he added.

Sohag Hasan and Omar Ali had 120,000 taka. When the robbers asked, they initially gave 20,000 taka. But the driver and his assistants "showed" the robbers that they had more money, Mr. Hasan claimed.

"Because I didn't want to pay, they threw me down and held me on my chest until I paid. And they kept me down until I got off the bus."

At that time, they couldn't speak, couldn't raise their heads, couldn't open their eyes. All they could hear were the screams and cries of two female passengers on the bus.

"Their seats were in the middle of the bus. There was a Hindu in between them, and her husband was with her," said Mr. Hasan.

The robbers first took everything the woman had.

"Then, right in front of us, a boy dragged the woman to the back seat and forcibly took her away. When her husband tried to stop her, he beat her up a lot," said Sohag Hasan.

"They also raped her. When they took her back, she was screaming a lot. They wouldn't let us go. We could only hear the screams. She was crying loudly. But there was nothing we could do there."

Pausing, he added, "No one would scream like that if they hadn't raped her."

Majnu Akand, a 73-year-old jaggery trader, was also on the bus that day. He also said, "The screaming we heard that night... the respect of our mother and sister... we had no words inside the car. They were hurting their dignity, they were fighting."

He also said that two women were the most tortured. One was about 20 years old, the other 25-30 years old.

Stating that "I don't know whether the two women complained of rape or not" at the police station, he said, "We know that two people were tortured inside the car."

Regarding the second woman being talked about, Sohag Hasan said, "Another woman, probably 25-30 years old. She took everything. She was two seats in front of us. They wouldn't let us look. She was touching different parts of her body. When we were going to protest again and again, they tried to beat us. They took only the Hindu girl in the back of the bus and sexually assaulted her".

Police denies rape:
Meanwhile, passengers say that at least one rape incident occurred on the bus that night. But the OCs of Baraigram and Mirzapur, both police stations, say they are not aware of any such incident.

Baraigram OC Sirajul Islam told the BBC, "I have not found any information about rape. No one has told me about any such incident or I have not found any witnesses."

And Mr. Islam was quoted by the Mirzapur OC as saying, "Many people are talking about rape. But I do not know anything about it. The Baraigram OC said that nothing like this happened."

47. Radical islamist teenage gang assaulted Hindu shop owner Reboti Gain and looted the shop. Adjacent temple was also vandalised and the caretakers of the temple were physically assaulted in Nazipur Upazilla, Pirojpur
Date of Incident:
February 15, 2025
Division: Barishal
District: Pirojpur
Upazilla: Nazirpur
Specific Location: Paschim Charpara
Details
A Hindu shop and temple was attacked in Nazirpur upazila of Pirojpur. At that time, 10 people were also injured while resisting the perpetrators.

"The attack took place at Samantakathi village of Matibhanga union on Friday night" said Nazirpur Police Station OC. Mahmud al-Farid Bhuiyan.

The injured were-Milan Gain, President of Samantakathi West Charpara Sri Sri Durga Temple, his wife named Irani Gain,and businessman Revati Gain and his wife Bithi Gain. They received treatment locally. The names of the others injured were not known.

The police had arrested three people in the incident. The arrested are Chhabbir Sheikh (25), Mustakin Sheikh (25) and Nayan Sheikh (25). They are residents of ward no 1 of the Mativanga Union.

The injured Revati Gain said, "A few days ago some of the local boys were sitting in my tea shop. They were using free WiFi for a long time.That's why,other customers did not find a place to sit. And for that reason I told them to get up. They talk to me about this later. They threaten me before their leaving.

It was supposed to be resolved at the local Shalis on Friday morning, but they did not come. Then at night, 9/20 people came to the shop. They beat me and looted the goods. When my wife Bithi tried to stop them, she was also beaten. She said that after attacking the shop, they also attacked the temple. At that time, the Durga idol of the temple was vandalized."

Milan Gain said, "We rushed to hear her screams during the attack on Revati Gain. The attackers started beating us. They broke the temple and idols when they had returned after the attack."

OC Mahmud Al-Farid Bhuiyan said, "Police sent to the scene after receiving the news. Three people were arrested and the rest were trying to arrest by police."

He said that Revati filed a case with the police station in the incident.

"Upon receiving the news, the restoration of the affected temples and idols has begun on Saturday morning", said Milon Gain, president of the temple commitee.
