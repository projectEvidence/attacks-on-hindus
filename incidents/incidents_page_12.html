169. Extremist Muslims attacked and looted the houses of local Hindu temple committee president and his Hindu neighbours in Lalpur, Natore
Date of Incident:
August 5, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Natore
Upazilla: Lalpur
Details
A group of people attacked the house of Dwipendranath Saha, the president of the Upazila Puja Celebration Council (পূজা উদ্‌যাপন পরিষদ) and the temple, who is also a school teacher, around 7:30 PM yesterday. The attackers broke down one of the gates of his house but failed to break the second one, so they shattered the windows from outside. Following this, they attacked the houses of his brother, Naresh Chandra Saha, and their neighbor, Shimul Sarkar. The attackers broke the doors, entered the houses, and carried out extensive vandalism.

Naresh Chandra claimed that the assailants looted over a hundred thousand taka, four tolas of gold jewelry, a laptop, mobile phones, and sacks of rice from his house.

Earlier, the attackers vandalized eight CCTV cameras at the Jotdaivaki Shiva and Kali Temple. Dwipendranath Saha told Prothom Alo over the phone that his house was located next to the temple. The attackers targeted his house right after attacking the temple. At that time, he informed the Lalpur police station, but an officer there said they would not be able to send police.
Involved Crimes

  • • Loot
  • • Robbery
