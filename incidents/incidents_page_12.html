169. The house of Narad Biswas was looted and attacked on August 7 in Lamaparkul village of Ichakals Union
Date of Incident:
August 6, 2024
Division: Sylhet
District: Sylhet
Upazilla: Companiganj
Specific Location: Lamaparkul, Ichakals union
Details
The attack took place at 2 pm on Wednesday (August 7) ​​at the house of Narad Biswas, son of the deceased Devendra Biswas, of Lamaparkul village in Ichakals union of the upazila. According to local sources, Narad Biswas, a prominent stone merchant of Lamaparkul village, is currently in jail in a case. Only his wife Sandhya Biswas lived in the house. Last Wednesday at 2 pm, a group of miscreants led by Sayeed Ahmed, president of the Ichakals union branch of Bangladesh Islami Chhatra Shibir, attacked Narad Biswas's house and vandalized it extensively. Fearing for his life, Sandhya Biswas fled through the back door of the house and took shelter in a neighboring house. At that time, the attackers broke the door of the house and entered, vandalized the furniture and looted gold ornaments and cash worth 43,000 taka from the cupboard. When the neighbors came out at the sound of the vandalism, the terrorists ran away. The neighbors said that Narad Biswas is a very peace-loving person. He is not involved in any kind of politics.

180. Extremist Muslims attacked and looted the houses of local Hindu temple committee president and his Hindu neighbours in Lalpur, Natore
Date of Incident:
August 5, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Natore
Upazilla: Lalpur
Details
A group of people attacked the house of Dwipendranath Saha, the president of the Upazila Puja Celebration Council (পূজা উদ্‌যাপন পরিষদ) and the temple, who is also a school teacher, around 7:30 PM yesterday. The attackers broke down one of the gates of his house but failed to break the second one, so they shattered the windows from outside. Following this, they attacked the houses of his brother, Naresh Chandra Saha, and their neighbor, Shimul Sarkar. The attackers broke the doors, entered the houses, and carried out extensive vandalism.

Naresh Chandra claimed that the assailants looted over a hundred thousand taka, four tolas of gold jewelry, a laptop, mobile phones, and sacks of rice from his house.

Earlier, the attackers vandalized eight CCTV cameras at the Jotdaivaki Shiva and Kali Temple. Dwipendranath Saha told Prothom Alo over the phone that his house was located next to the temple. The attackers targeted his house right after attacking the temple. At that time, he informed the Lalpur police station, but an officer there said they would not be able to send police.
