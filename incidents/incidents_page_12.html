167. The land of Bharat Das and his brother Shambhu Das was forcefully taken by local influential people in Jhenaidah in the name playground
Date of Incident:
September 6, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Jhenaidah
Upazilla: Maheshpur
Details
Local influential people have been accused of encroaching on the land of a minority community and building a playground in Maheshpur, Jhenaidah.

The victim family held a press conference at the Jhenaidah Press Club on Saturday morning to protest the incident and demand the return of the land. They alleged in the press conference that Bharat Das and his brother Shambhu Das are the owners of land plots 104 and 103 of Khatian 2867 of Hamidpur Mouza No. 108 of Gopalpur village in Maheshpur upazila. They inherited the land and owned it. Their family has been cultivating this land for almost a hundred years. But when the land was mistakenly recorded in the government's Khatian during the field survey, they later filed a case and got the land back. After the fall of the Awami League government, some influential people tried to cut the crops planted on their land and seize the land. When they filed a case in the court regarding this, the court issued a ban. Ignoring this, on Thursday, Maheshpur Municipality Councilor Babul Hossain, Ikramul, Bipul, Khaled Hossain, Jinnah and a few others from that village destroyed the crops and occupied the land.

Date of Incident:
August 31, 2024
Division: Sylhet
District: Sylhet
Upazilla: Sylhet Sadar
Specific Location: Mirjajangal Tanim Tower, Jamtola road
Details
A visa consultancy firm in Sylhet has been a victim of extortion. An extortion gang threatened the director of the firm, saying that the business would not be able to run unless regular extortion was received.

The incident took place on September 1 at Mirjajangal Tanim Tower on Jamtala Road. In this incident, the director of the firm, Bipasha Chowdhury, filed a general diary with the Sylhet Kotwali Police Station on September 10, seeking legal intervention. Its number is 797.

In the diary, she mentioned that on the day of the incident, at approximately 3 pm, 4/5 unknown people entered my firm named SBS Migration and demanded a cash extortion of 50,000 taka from me. When I expressed my inability to accept their offer, the extortion gang called me a minority and warned me that I would have to pay an extortion every month to run the business regularly. Otherwise, they threatened to close the firm and flee.
