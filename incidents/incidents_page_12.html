166. Business owned by Barun Kumar Ghosh looted during broad daylight in Satkhira
Date of Incident:
August 5, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Satkhira
Upazilla: Kaliganj
Specific Location: Bharashimla Union
Details
On August 6, at around 10:30 am, more than a hundred armed people returning from the BNP procession on the south side of Kankshiali Bridge broke the shutters of “Ma Lakshmi Bhandar” and “Ma Lakshmi Muri Bhandar” and entered. First, they vandalized 10 CCTV cameras inside the mill. Then they looted 500 bags of 50 kg Atap rice, 700 bags of 28 rice of 50 and 25 kg, 1,100 bags of 50 kg coarse rice, 300 bags of miniket, 250 bags (25 kg) Basmati, 300 bags of fresh chopped rice (for frying Muri), 16 bags of gold, 750 bags of hybrid rice, 350 bags of wheat bran, 750 bags of Atap (polished) like kudra, 900 bags of kudra, 750 bags of corn, 200 bags of 28 paddy, 300 bags of coarse paddy, 350 bags of non-basmati paddy, 250 bags of peasant feed, along with the Muri mill, crush mill, shredder mill, rice mill, a tube well, 17 peas, an iron chest, and various other items. The attackers broke open the electric meter and took various items, including wires.

174. Extremist Muslims attacked and looted the houses of local Hindu temple committee president and his Hindu neighbours in Lalpur, Natore
Date of Incident:
August 5, 2024
Division: Rajshahi
District: Natore
Upazilla: Lalpur
Details
A group of people attacked the house of Dwipendranath Saha, the president of the Upazila Puja Celebration Council (পূজা উদ্‌যাপন পরিষদ) and the temple, who is also a school teacher, around 7:30 PM yesterday. The attackers broke down one of the gates of his house but failed to break the second one, so they shattered the windows from outside. Following this, they attacked the houses of his brother, Naresh Chandra Saha, and their neighbor, Shimul Sarkar. The attackers broke the doors, entered the houses, and carried out extensive vandalism.

Naresh Chandra claimed that the assailants looted over a hundred thousand taka, four tolas of gold jewelry, a laptop, mobile phones, and sacks of rice from his house.

Earlier, the attackers vandalized eight CCTV cameras at the Jotdaivaki Shiva and Kali Temple. Dwipendranath Saha told Prothom Alo over the phone that his house was located next to the temple. The attackers targeted his house right after attacking the temple. At that time, he informed the Lalpur police station, but an officer there said they would not be able to send police.
