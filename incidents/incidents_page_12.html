166. Arun Das a victim of land grabbing from Awami League leader Hasnat Abdullah, Hasnat grabbed a lot of land in the 16 years of the previous Awami League government by using influence and fear
Date of Incident:
September 25, 2024
Division: Barishal
District: Barishal
Upazilla: Gaurnadi
Specific Location: Dakhsin Palardi
Details
It is alleged that Hasanat Abdullah grabbed a lot of land in the 16 years of the previous Awami League government by using influence and fear, sometimes at a nominal price, sometimes by force. Abul Hasanat Abdullah is the President of Barisal District Awami League.

There are also allegations against Hasanat of grabbing land from minority communities. Arun Das (72), the owner of the traditional 'The Laxman Das Circus', alleged that Hasanat Abdullah had his eye on the land of their under-construction 'Leela Cinema Hall' in Dakshin Palradi Mouza, next to the Dhaka-Barisal highway. He put pressure on him to get the land deed. When he did not agree, Awami League leader Abul Hossain Mollah and local Selim Hawladar offered to sell the land, but he refused.

Later, Hasanat Abdullah used the administration to stop their circus show. This caused them financial losses. Since the circus was closed for a long time, it became difficult to bear the cost of food for the artists and animals. Then he was forced to deed 34 percent of the land, including the building under construction. Hasanat Abdullah gave only 3.6 million taka for the property worth about 1.5 crore taka. Arun Das lamented, "It didn't end here, Hasanat Abdullah again pressured me to write down the dighi on 96 percent of my land. But when I refused, Lutfar Rahman, the general secretary of Gournadi Upazila Chhatra League, threatened to kill me. After that, I stopped going to Gournadi out of fear for my life."

168. Neighbour Muslims raped and killed a Hindu Housewife; and dumped her dead body in septic tank
Date of Incident:
September 22, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Jashore
Upazilla: Abhaynagar
Specific Location: Devpara area of ​​Bhatpara village in Baghutia union of Abhaynagar upazila
Details
Housewife Sabita Rani Dey (50) went missing in September last year while collecting cow fodder in Abhaynagar, Jessore. Her body was recovered from a neighbor's septic tank 33 hours later. The matter came to light only recently when the police received the viscera report after about five months. The report states that the housewife was raped and strangled to death.

On the evening of September 24, 2024, the police recovered the body of housewife Sabita Rani Dey from the Devpara area of ​​Bhatpara village in Baghutia union of Abhaynagar upazila. The body was sent to the morgue of the 250-bed Jessore Hospital for autopsy. Samples were collected for viscera testing and sent to the CID forensic lab. The police recently received the viscera report.

A day after the body was recovered, on September 25, housewife Sabita Rani's husband Milan Kumar Dey filed a case with Abhaynagar police station. The case named Niyamul Sheikh (26), his father Ramzan Sheikh (49) and uncle Yunus Sheikh (41) of Devpara area of ​​neighboring Bhatpara village. The police have also arrested three people. Among the three, Niyamul Sheikh is in jail. The other two have been released on bail.

According to the case sources, the housewife went to the bamboo garden near her house on the morning of September 23 to collect cow feed. After that, when she did not return home, her family members and neighbors searched for her. About 33 hours after she went missing, her body was recovered from the septic tank of Niyamul Sheikh's toilet adjacent to the bamboo garden near her house.

Locals said that the husband of the traditional housewife is a shingara, puri and tea shop owner. He has a shop in the local Bhatpara market. Their two daughters are married. A husband and wife lived in the house.

When asked about this, the investigating officer of the case, Bhatpara Police Investigation Center Sub-Inspector (SI) Nazrul Islam, told Ajker Patrika that the viscera report of the housewife's body was received a few days ago. The report states that she was raped and strangled to death. After the investigation, the charge sheet of the case will be sent to the court.

170. Miscreants have sent threatening letters to various temples demanding money ahead of the upcoming Durga Puja, the main festival of Hindus in Khulna
Date of Incident:
September 21, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Khulna
Upazilla: Dacope
Specific Location: Chalna, Sutarkhali, Kailashganj and Bajua
Details
Miscreants have sent letters to various temples demanding extortion in connection with the upcoming Durga Puja, the main festival of Hindus in Khulna. In particular, the leaders of the Puja celebration committees of various temples in Dakop upazila, a Hindu-dominated area, have received letters demanding extortion of Tk 5 lakh. At the same time, the letters have threatened that they will be beaten if they tell the administration or journalists.

These letters started coming to the presidents and general secretaries of various temples last Wednesday. After receiving the letters, panic has spread among the Hindu community, priests and temple committee members in the area. A general diary has been filed with the police station on behalf of some temples in Dakop upazila. Meanwhile, the leaders of the celebration committees have said that some temples have decided not to hold pujas after receiving letters demanding extortion and threats from various temples.
