9. Hindu Cremation Site Bulldozed Under UNO’s Orders for Cow Market Construction; Demands for His Removal Intensify
Date of Incident:
May 15, 2025
Division: Mymensingh
District: Mymensingh
Upazilla: Ishwarganj
Specific Location: Beside the Kachamatia River, adjacent to Uchakhila Bazar
Details
In a deeply distressing turn of events, the 150-year-old Hindu cremation ground located in Uchakhila Union of Ishwarganj Upazila, Mymensingh, has come under serious threat as local authorities allegedly began filling the site with sand to set up a cattle market. Members of the Hindu Sanatan community have accused the Upazila Nirbahi Officer (UNO), Ershadul Ahmed, of orchestrating the destruction of this sacred site, which has served as the final resting place for generations of local Hindus. The community has condemned this move as a gross violation of religious rights and heritage, emphasizing that the cremation ground holds deep emotional and spiritual significance, where countless ancestors were cremated and rituals are still regularly performed.

On Sunday, hundreds of local Hindus staged a protest by blocking the Mymensingh–Kishoreganj highway for over an hour, demanding the immediate removal of the UNO and a halt to the desecration of the cremation ground. Protesters revealed that not only was the site being buried in sand, but the construction of a Kali temple and essential cremation infrastructure were also being destroyed in the process. Despite court records and official documents verifying the land as a cremation ground, government records in 1984–85 reclassified it as public (khas) land, which later enabled authorities to reclaim it. However, the Hindu community had regained access to the site in recent years and was in the process of building a boundary wall with its own funds when the new encroachment began.

Though local officials have attempted to downplay the incident as a misunderstanding, the timing and nature of the demolition—especially during the construction of a temple—are seen by the Hindu community as yet another instance of systemic communal discrimination. Community leaders have issued a strong warning that unless the order is reversed and the UNO removed within 24 hours, they will launch larger protests to protect their sacred land and religious rights.
