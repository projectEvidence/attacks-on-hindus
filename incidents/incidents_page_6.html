81. Ancestral land of Satyaranjan Patni was forcefully grabbed defying court order in Chailabunia Village, Galachipa
Date of Incident:
December 7, 2024
Division: Barishal
District: Patuakhali
Upazilla: Galachipa
Details
In a disturbing example of disregard for legal justice and minority rights, a Hindu family in Chhaila Buniya village, Galachipa Upazila of Patuakhali, has accused local musclemen of forcibly seizing their land in defiance of a court verdict. Victim Satyaranjan Patni raised the alarm during a press conference at the Patuakhali Press Club on Sunday, joined by his family and legal counsel.

According to Satyaranjan, a 2021 inheritance case over 4.84 acres of ancestral land was settled in their favor by a local court in February 2023. An appeal was filed by the opposing party but was rejected by the Senior District Judge’s Court. Following that, government officials officially demarcated and handed over the land to the rightful owners.

Yet, in blatant contempt of the law, Md. Abdur Rob Hawlader and Mofez Uddin Sikder have allegedly taken over the land by force. On Sunday, they even harvested paddy from the disputed field, ignoring both the court order and the police. Despite repeated complaints, local authorities and even the military camp reportedly failed to act.

The accused claim to have bought the land from women in Satyaranjan’s extended family, but under Hindu family law, such female inheritance claims are invalid in this context. Still, these false claims are being used to justify the ongoing occupation and intimidation.

Satyaranjan and his family now live in fear, constantly receiving threats. Their lawyer stated, "My clients legally marked the land with court-decreed boundaries over a month ago, yet the occupiers continue to dominate with brute power. The family is terrified and urgently seeks protection and justice."

This incident reflects a worrying trend of land grabbing targeting religious minorities, where even court decrees fail to protect victims in the face of political influence and law enforcement inaction.

82. Anupam Sen Resigns Amid Protests from students motivated by religious hatred in Chattogram’s GEC Campus from the post of VC at Premier University
Date of Incident:
December 6, 2024
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Pachlaish
Specific Location: GEC Campus of Premier University, Nizam Road
Details
In a deeply troubling development, Dr. Anupam Sen — a revered Hindu academic, prominent sociologist, and recipient of Bangladesh’s prestigious Ekushey Padak — was effectively forced to resign from his position as Vice-Chancellor of Premier University, Chattogram, under mounting pressure and aggressive actions by a group of politically-charged student agitators.

Though Dr. Sen formally submitted his resignation on Friday night citing age-related reasons, the context of the decision paints a far darker picture. For days, a faction of students operating under the banner “General Students’ Anti-Discrimination Movement, Premier University” staged escalating demonstrations demanding the removal of Dr. Sen, the Pro-Vice-Chancellor, and the Treasurer. Their actions included locking the gates of three campuses, including the main GEC campus — an aggressive tactic that disrupted academic operations and created a climate of intimidation.

The protesters accused Dr. Sen of harboring political affiliations, labeling him as an “oppressor” and “enemy of the revolution” due to his advisory role in the ruling Awami League and his perceived opposition to the so-called July revolution. These accusations, devoid of any academic or administrative justification, were clearly politically motivated and rooted in ideological intolerance.

Despite his decades-long contributions to education and social science, Dr. Sen, a member of Bangladesh’s Hindu minority, was vilified and hounded into resignation. His 50+ years of teaching excellence and his leadership since 2006 at Premier University were cast aside by a radical faction unwilling to tolerate diverse views or respect his legacy.

Dr. Sen’s resignation was announced during an academic council meeting, where he reportedly shared his decision with department heads under palpable pressure from the ongoing protests and the threat of more “intensified” agitation planned for Saturday.

This incident is a sobering reminder of how a mob-driven atmosphere — fueled by hostility, intolerance, and political opportunism — can unjustly topple even the most respected figures in academia. It reflects not only a personal loss for Dr. Sen, but a dangerous precedent where reason is drowned out by radicalism, and a Hindu scholar is forced out not for wrongdoing, but for who he is and what he represents.
