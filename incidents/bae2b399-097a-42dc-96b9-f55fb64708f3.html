67. Ancient Vishnu Idol Weighing 19 kg recovered from the house of Yakub Sheikh of Debraj village in Ghatmajhi Union, Madaripur Sadar Upazila
Date of Incident:
February 19, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Madaripur
Upazilla: Madaripur Sadar
Specific Location: Ghatmajhi (ঘটমাঝি) Union
Details
Madaripur Sadar Police have recovered a stone Vishnu idol weighing about 19 kg from the Debraj area of Ghatmajhi Union in Madaripur Sadar Upazila. On Wednesday evening, the idol was recovered from the house of Yakub Sheikh of Debraj village in Ghatmajhi Union under Sadar Upazila. Police and locals believe that the idol is made of hard stone and represents Lord Vishnu. According to local and police sources, Yakub Sheikh had been digging soil from a pond for two days to build a new house. While digging deep, he found some objects resembling stones covered in soil. Later, his nephew lifted them out and washed them with water, revealing that they were stone idols. He kept the idol at home, along with three broken pieces believed to be parts of the whole statue. Some locals said it was made of black stone, while others claimed it was a type of hard stone. As news spread, hundreds of people gathered at the house of farmer Yakub Sheikh at night to see the idol. Later, the police were informed. Sadar Police Station quickly arrived to protect what may be state-owned antiquities. They recovered the statue and its pieces and took them to the police station. Farmer Yakub Sheikh said, "While digging soil with a shovel to build my son's house, the statues came out of the pond along with the soil. After that, with the help of locals, we informed the police, and they came and took the statues and pieces to the police station. We can't say anything for sure without verifying whether these are Kashti stones or not." Sadar Police Station Inspector Mahmudul Hasan said, "Upon receiving the news, we recovered three pieces of a statue from the house of Yakub Sheikh in Debraj village of Sadar Upazila. Looking at the stones, it seems that they are Kashti stone statues. However, whether these are truly Kashti stones can only be confirmed by the Department of Archaeology through proper testing. Further legal action is in process." Madaripur Sadar Model Police Station Officer-in-Charge (OC) Md. Mokchedur Rahman said, "Three pieces of stone idols have been recovered from the house of Yakub Sheikh in the Debraj area of Ghatmajhi Union under Madaripur Sadar Police Station. Their total weight is 18 kg 888 grams. Experts from the Department of Archaeology will examine them to confirm if they are Kashti stones. If confirmed, the idols will be handed over to the department."
