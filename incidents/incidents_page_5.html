64. Disguised Robbers Posing as Hindus Arrested While Plotting Attack on Hindu Home in Mirzapur, Tangail
Date of Incident:
December 27, 2024
Division: Dhaka
District: Tangail
Upazilla: Mirzapur
Details
In a chilling attempt that combined criminal intent with religious deception, police in Mirzapur, Tangail, thwarted a planned armed robbery targeting a Hindu household, arresting 10 members of an inter-district robbery gang, including 8 women, on the night of Friday, December 27, 2024.

According to Tangail Superintendent of Police Saiful Islam Santu, the robbers had traveled from Salna, Gazipur, in a microbus and were preparing to raid a Hindu family's home in the Sohagpur area. Shockingly, the female gang members had adorned themselves with traditional Hindu symbols like shankha (shell bangles) and sindoor (vermillion) to impersonate devout Hindu women, aiming to gain the trust of the household and carry out the crime undetected.

The gang was intercepted by the Detective Branch (North) under the leadership of Officer-in-Charge ABMS Dohar, and all 10 members were arrested on the spot. Their vehicle was also seized. A case has been filed by Sub-Inspector Monir Hossain at Mirzapur Police Station.

The arrested individuals include:
Surat Mia (38), Taslima (32), Md. Selim Sarkar (37), Asma Begum (30), Aleya (37), Sonia Akhter (21), Rifa Akhter (26), Aklima Begum (40), Selina Begum (32), and Julekha Begum (31).

According to police, the gang is known for meticulously scouting targets, gathering information beforehand, and using elaborate disguises to infiltrate homes, particularly those belonging to vulnerable families.

This incident has deeply alarmed the local Hindu community, as it not only represents a criminal attack but also exploits religious identity to facilitate harm, intensifying feelings of insecurity among minorities.

Authorities have pledged continued investigation to uncover the masterminds behind the operation, but Hindu residents are calling for increased security and stronger protection of minority neighborhoods, especially from criminals who exploit religious symbols to commit acts of violence.

Date of Incident:
December 25, 2024
Division: Chattogram
District: Comilla
Upazilla: Debidwar
Details
In a horrific act of communal violence, an elderly Hindu couple—Nitai Bhowmik and his wife—were brutally attacked and killed in their home in Barkamta village, Debidwar Upazila, Cumilla District, on the night of December 26, 2024, at around 8:15 PM.

The attackers, reportedly led by Md. Titu Swarnakar, son of Md. Yusuf Swarnakar, were part of a local Muslim gang that allegedly stormed the couple’s residence with knives and sticks, aiming to kill and loot their property. Both victims died on the spot due to the severe nature of the assault.

Shockingly, both the victims and the perpetrators are from the same village, yet instead of legal action being pursued, local BNP leaders are allegedly attempting to suppress the case through informal arbitration (salish)—a move widely condemned by human rights observers as an attempt to deny justice and protect the attackers.

Community members fear the assault was motivated not just by greed, but by religious hatred, as Hindu families in rural Bangladesh increasingly face targeted violence and systemic marginalization. Calls for justice have intensified as the family and locals demand criminal prosecution and government intervention, warning against any political protection or backdoor settlements.

This attack adds to the growing list of violent crimes against minorities that are often buried under silence, social pressure, or political influence—leaving victims without justice and communities in fear.

68. Hindu Woman UP Member Basana Mallik (45) Allegedly Gang-Raped, Poisoned to Death in Narail Sadar – Family Cries for Justice
Date of Incident:
December 23, 2024
Division: Khulna
District: Narail
Police Station: Narail
Specific Location: Daulatpur, Maizpara Union, Narail Sadar
Details
In a horrifying act of gendered and communal violence, Basana Mallik (45), a Hindu woman and elected Union Parishad member of Maijpara Union (Wards 1, 2, and 3) in Narail Sadar Upazila, was allegedly gang-raped by a group of men and later poisoned to death. The incident has sent shockwaves through the local community, with widespread calls for justice.

According to the victim's family, Basana Mallik was lured to Daulatpur Bazar on Tuesday, after receiving a phone call from a local youth named Rajibul, asking her to collect some dues. She later visited a female acquaintance’s house in Daulatpur village, where several men—Faruk Mollah, Chanchal, Shafiqul, and Kibria—arrived, questioned her, and after Rajibul fled the scene, forcibly dragged her into a nearby orchard.

There, she was brutally gang-raped, and the assailants allegedly recorded the assault. They then threatened to release the video and demanded two lakh taka from her. When she resisted, they forcefully poured poison into her mouth, leaving her gravely ill.

Her husband reported that she returned home vomiting uncontrollably and eventually lost the ability to speak. She was rushed to Jessore Sadar Hospital, where she succumbed to her injuries on Thursday night. Before her death, she reportedly disclosed the names of her attackers to her only son.

One suspect, Faruk Mollah, has been arrested in Magura, while police say others will be detained soon. A rape and murder case is being prepared, and the police are treating the allegations with seriousness, pending forensic and postmortem results.

Local union members, including Akhtar Mollah and Piyari Begum, confirmed that community members widely believe Basana was raped and murdered. Villagers are demanding a fair investigation and exemplary punishment for the perpetrators.

This tragic case reflects a disturbing pattern of violence against women—especially from marginalized Hindu communities—facing threats both as public representatives and religious minorities. The community now awaits action not only from law enforcement, but from the nation’s conscience.
