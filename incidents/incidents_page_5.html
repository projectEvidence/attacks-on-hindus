63. A Repeat Offender Md. Mirajuddin Arrested for Vandalising Saraswati Idol at Faridpur Temple Days Before Puja
Date of Incident:
January 30, 2025
Division: Dhaka
District: Faridpur
Upazilla: Faridpur Sadar
Details
Saraswati Idol Vandalised at Kali Temple in Faridpur.

A Saraswati idol was vandalised at a Kali temple in Faridpur just three days before the start of Saraswati Puja. A youth has been arrested in connection with the incident. Saraswati Puja will be celebrated on February 3.

The incident took place at the Kali temple in the Bhatilakshmipur area of the district around 11 p.m. on Friday. Faridpur Kotwali police arrested the youth around 12:30 a.m. and took him to the police station.

Police identified the arrested youth as Md. Mirajuddin. He earns a living by driving an easybike. Miraj, a resident of Bajedpur village in Basantpur Union of Rajbari Sadar Upazila, currently lives in a rented house at the Rajbari Road intersection.

Ramchandra Malo, a member of the temple committee, said, “Our temple is beside the road in Bhatilakshmipur. An easybike stopped in front of the temple shortly before 11 p.m. on Friday night. The driver then entered the verandah of the temple, pushed the Saraswati idol, and vandalised it. Later, locals caught him and handed him over to the police.”

Earlier, the same individual (Mirajuddin) had broken the Saraswati idol at Faridpur Sri Sri Radhagovinda ISKCON temple. Ramchandra Malo claimed that he was let off as "insane" after the incident on February 7, 2024.

Meanwhile, a 26-second CCTV footage provided by the temple committee has confirmed the incident.

Samar Mondal, a student of Government Rajendra College, said, “The idol was made by a potter under a contract of Tk 6,000. We students, who stay in a hostel next to this temple, had planned to celebrate Saraswati Puja together. I had also invited many friends. But the idol was broken at such a time that there is no longer enough time to repair it.”

Faridpur Kotwali Police Station Officer-in-Charge (OC) Asaduzzaman said, “The arrested youth is currently in police custody.”

Faridpur Additional Superintendent of Police (Crime and Operations) Sailen Chakma added, “A member of the temple committee is preparing to file a case. After the case is filed, the young man will be formally arrested and sent to court.”

75. Disguised Robbers Posing as Hindus Arrested While Plotting Attack on Hindu Home in Mirzapur, Tangail
Date of Incident:
December 27, 2024
Division: Dhaka
District: Tangail
Upazilla: Mirzapur
Details
In a chilling attempt that combined criminal intent with religious deception, police in Mirzapur, Tangail, thwarted a planned armed robbery targeting a Hindu household, arresting 10 members of an inter-district robbery gang, including 8 women, on the night of Friday, December 27, 2024.

According to Tangail Superintendent of Police Saiful Islam Santu, the robbers had traveled from Salna, Gazipur, in a microbus and were preparing to raid a Hindu family's home in the Sohagpur area. Shockingly, the female gang members had adorned themselves with traditional Hindu symbols like shankha (shell bangles) and sindoor (vermillion) to impersonate devout Hindu women, aiming to gain the trust of the household and carry out the crime undetected.

The gang was intercepted by the Detective Branch (North) under the leadership of Officer-in-Charge ABMS Dohar, and all 10 members were arrested on the spot. Their vehicle was also seized. A case has been filed by Sub-Inspector Monir Hossain at Mirzapur Police Station.

The arrested individuals include:
Surat Mia (38), Taslima (32), Md. Selim Sarkar (37), Asma Begum (30), Aleya (37), Sonia Akhter (21), Rifa Akhter (26), Aklima Begum (40), Selina Begum (32), and Julekha Begum (31).

According to police, the gang is known for meticulously scouting targets, gathering information beforehand, and using elaborate disguises to infiltrate homes, particularly those belonging to vulnerable families.

This incident has deeply alarmed the local Hindu community, as it not only represents a criminal attack but also exploits religious identity to facilitate harm, intensifying feelings of insecurity among minorities.

Authorities have pledged continued investigation to uncover the masterminds behind the operation, but Hindu residents are calling for increased security and stronger protection of minority neighborhoods, especially from criminals who exploit religious symbols to commit acts of violence.
