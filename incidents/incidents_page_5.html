Details

66. Targeted attack in Hindu residential area called "Sevak colony" of Chittagong. Houses, businesses, and temples were vandalized, looted and set on fire
Date of Incident:
November 25, 2024
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Specific Location: Sevak Colony, Bandel Road (সেবক কলোনি, বান্ডেল রোড)
Details
Houses, businesses, Gopal Mandir and Shitala Mandir in Bandel Road Sevak Colony in Chittagong have been attacked, vandalized and set on fire. Among them, Gopal Mandir was set on fire. The attack was carried out in protest against the stabbing of lawyer Saiful Islam Alif in the lane of Rangam Convention Hall on Tuesday evening, November 26. Although the police administration inspected the scene of the attack on the temple, the police station did not register a case in this incident. It is alleged that they have also threatened to take action if they file a case!

A few days after the incident, on Sunday (December 1), Sarozmin saw that 4 houses and 4 shops had been burnt outside the gate of Sevak Colony. Among them, a decoration shop was burnt down with a case closed.

Chittagong Harijan Samiti President Bishnu Das said that when they set fire to the Gopal temple and vandalized the Maa Shitala temple that evening, the police had accused the men who had come out of Sevak Colony to protect the temple after looking at the CCTV camera footage. However, the video of who killed them has gone viral on social media. The lives of innocent people have ended. Most of our men are homeless due to fear of arrest. We want the real criminals to be tried, but what does it mean to accuse them en masse? We have been wronged because we are a Hindu minority.

73. Government administration, Rangpur DC and Police Super obstructs peaceful protest of Hindus against country wide violence on Hindus. Administration changed previously permitted venue at the eleventh hour and imposing restrictions.
Date of Incident:
November 22, 2024
Division: Rangpur
District: Rangpur
Upazilla: Kaunia
Details
Hindus were attacked in Kawani while they were on their way to a rally in Rangpur. Three people were seriously injured in the attack. They were admitted to a hospital under the Rangpur Police Superintendent and treated. There have been allegations of an attack on a bus in Kurigram on their way to participate in a rally in Rangpur to raise the demands of the Hindu community. Hindu community leaders have claimed that 20 people were injured in the incident on Friday afternoon. They alleged that some of the buses that were reserved for participating in the rally were stopped and turned back. After stopping some buses, the passengers blocked the road and protested, but the law enforcement agencies allowed them to go.

Earlier, the ‘Bangladesh Sammilit Sanatani Jagran Jote’ did not get permission to hold a divisional rally at the Zilla School ground in Rangpur city with eight demands. However, the organization has been asked to hold a rally at Mahiganj Degree College ground, far from the city, today, Friday afternoon.

After the student uprising on August 5, the 'Sanatan Jagran Mancha' has been protesting all over the country demanding justice for the torture, oppression and attacks on the Hindu community and monasteries. On November 17, Bangladesh Sanatan Jagran Mancha and Bangladesh Sammilit Minanglaghu Jote jointly launched the 'Sammilit Sanatani Jagran Jote'. A Rangpur divisional rally is scheduled to be held under the banner of this alliance today.

Sanatan Jagran Mancha Rangpur coordinator Dipankar Bhattacharya told Prothom Alo that they have sought permission from the district administration to hold a rally at the district school ground. But they have not been given permission to use the district school ground. They have been asked to hold the rally at Mahiganj Degree College ground, four kilometers from Rangpur city.
