12. Communal Attack on Ganesh Puja Celebration in Joynagar, Nabiganj Leaves Three Injured
Date of Incident:
May 11, 2025
Division: Sylhet
District: Habiganj
Upazilla: Nabiganj
Specific Location: House of Chonu Das
Details
On the night of May 11, 2025, a communal attack took place during a Hindu religious and cultural event in Joynagar village under Nabiganj municipality, Habiganj. As Hindu devotees gathered to celebrate Ganesh Puja — a tradition upheld by the ‘Joynagar Apyayan Sangha’ for over a decade — a group of Muslim youths stormed the venue, violently attacking participants and desecrating the puja mandap. The assault left at least three members of the Hindu community seriously injured: Shreebas Das (32), Noyon Das (18), and Momota Das (30), all of whom were hospitalized at the Nabiganj Upazila Health Complex.

The incident occurred when a group of young men from nearby villages, including Mahmudur Rahman (20) and Tanvir Mia (19), reportedly misbehaved during the cultural segment of the religious event held at the home of Hindu resident Chonu Das. When the organizers asked them to leave due to inappropriate conduct, they returned shortly afterward with 15–20 attackers wielding local weapons. They launched a coordinated assault on the worshippers and destroyed furniture and decorations in the sacred space. The attack disrupted the religious proceedings and struck fear among the minority Hindu community, who view this as a deliberate communal assault meant to intimidate and suppress their cultural and religious rights.

In response, a police case was filed by Chonu Das, naming six identified individuals and several unknown attackers. Nabiganj police have since arrested Mahmudur Rahman and Tanvir Mia, and further investigations are ongoing. Despite the arrests, the Hindu community remains anxious and demands strong legal action against all perpetrators and assurance of security during future religious observances. This attack is yet another alarming example of the growing vulnerability of Hindus in Bangladesh, even during peaceful religious gatherings.
