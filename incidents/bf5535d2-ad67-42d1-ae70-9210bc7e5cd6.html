59. Amid widespread kidnapping and blood money demand, another Hindu businessman Uttam Das(37) from Brahmanbaria is missing
Date of Incident:
January 8, 2025
Division: Chattogram
District: Chattogram
Upazilla: Kasba
Police Station: Kotwali, CMP
Details
After the regime change since 5th August 2024, a spree of kidnapping and blood money demand has been noticed across the country. The latest victim is a businessman Uttam Das (37).
Businessman Uttam Das (37), a resident of Kasba upazila in Brahmanbaria, has been missing for the past three days after traveling to Chattogram for business purposes. He went to Chattogram last Thursday, and since Friday night, his family has been unable to contact him. Both of Uttam Das's mobile phones have been switched off. The matter has been reported to both Kotwali Police Station in Chattogram and Kasba Police Station in Brahmanbaria

Uttam Das is the son of Gouranga Das from Jajira village in Kuti Union of Kasba Upazila, Brahmanbaria. By profession, he is a dried fish (shutki) trader. According to his family members, he often traveled to Chattogram for business purposes. His family has been deeply worried since they have been unable to trace him for the past three days.

Uttam Das's nephew, Satya Ranjan Das, said that Uttam went to Chattogram on Thursday for business purposes. His last communication with family members was around 8 PM on Friday, during which he informed them that he would be staying at a residential hotel. Since then, they have been unable to contact him.

At the time of his disappearance, Uttam Das had two iPhones with him, along with a gold bracelet and a ring. After losing contact, the family first informed Kasba Police Station and later notified Kotwali Police Station in Chattogram. A written complaint has already been filed with Kotwali Police Station. Meanwhile, family members are still in Chattogram searching for him.

Uttam Das’s younger brother, Sushanta Das, mentioned that his elder brother was involved in wholesale trading of fish.
